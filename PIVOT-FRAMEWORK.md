# Pivot Framework — Book Digitization Platform

This framework is designed specifically for **Origin Eyes Inc.** as you transition from a mission-driven digitization project for the Criterion Educational Foundation into a scalable, potentially commercial or high-impact non-profit enterprise.

---

### 1. PIVOT DECISION TRIGGERS
Since your model is **transactional ($5/book)**, your primary risks are low margins, high customer acquisition costs (CAC), and scaling bottlenecks.

| # | Metric | Threshold | Measurement Period | Confidence Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | **Unit Margin** | Net profit per book < $1.50 | 3 Consecutive Months | 95% |
| 2 | **CAC to LTV Ratio** | CAC > 3x LTV (Lifetime Value) | 6 Months | 90% |
| 3 | **Throughput Ceiling** | Max books/month < 50% of target | 2 Months | 85% |
| 4 | **OCR Accuracy Rate** | Error rate > 4% in final output | 1 Month | 95% |
| 5 | **Repeat Purchase Rate** | < 15% of clients order a 2nd batch | 4 Months | 80% |
| 6 | **Sales Cycle Length** | > 90 days from lead to transaction | 6 Months | 75% |
| 7 | **Customer Concentration**| > 80% of revenue from 1 client | Ongoing | 90% |
| 8 | **Lead Conversion** | < 2% conversion from inquiry to order | 3 Months | 85% |
| 9 | **Operational Cost** | Compute/Labor cost > $3.50/book | 3 Months | 90% |
| 10 | **Market Velocity** | MoM volume growth < 5% | 4 Months | 70% |

### 2. PIVOT TYPE ANALYSIS
*Specific to the Book Digitization Platform context.*

| Pivot Type | Description | Feasibility | Strategic Logic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Zoom-in** | Focus solely on "High-Fidelity OCR for Rare/Old Manuscripts." | **High** | Moves from commodity digitization to a specialized, high-value niche. |
| **Zoom-out** | Digitization becomes a module inside a "Digital Library Management System." | **Medium** | Increases LTV by providing the software to *manage* the books you digitize. |
| **Customer Segment** | Move from Religious Foundations to Law Firms or Medical Archives. | **High** | Shifts from low-budget non-profits to high-budget professional services. |
| **Customer Need** | Instead of "digitizing," you provide "Searchable AI Knowledge Bases." | **High** | Solves the problem of *finding* information, not just *storing* it. |
| **Channel** | Partnering with scanning hardware companies or library wholesalers. | **Medium** | Avoids direct sales; you become the "Intel Inside" for digitization hardware. |
| **Revenue Model** | From $5/book to a monthly "Digital Archive" subscription (SaaS). | **Medium** | Stabilizes cash flow; moves from transactional to recurring revenue. |

### 4. STRATEGIC OPTIONALITY MAP

**[Market Risk (High $\rightarrow$ Low)]**
**[Execution Risk (Low $\rightarrow$ High)]**

*   **Quadrant 1: Low Execution / Low Market Risk (The "Safe Bets")**
    *   *B2B Managed Service* (You already have the tech/process).
*   **Quadrant 2: High Execution / Low Market Risk (The "Scalable Giants")**
    *   *AI Training Data Provider* (High demand, but requires massive data volume/cleanliness).
    *   *AI Search API* (High tech hurdle, but huge market).
*   **Quadrant 3: Low Execution / High Market Risk (The "Niche Plays")**
    *   *Content Licensing* (Easy to do if you have rights, but hard to find buyers).
*   **Quadrant 4: High Execution / High Market Risk (The "Moonshots")**
    *   *Specialized EdTech SaaS* (Requires massive product build and user adoption).

### 5. PIVOT EXECUTION PLAYBOOK (8-WEEK PLAN)

*   **Weeks 1-2: Validation (The "Truth" Phase)**
    *   Analyze Trigger Data (from Section 1).
    *   Conduct 20 "Problem Interviews" with potential new segments (e.g., Law firms).
    *   *Output:* A "Pivot Thesis" document.
*   **Weeks 3-4: MVP (The "Smoke Test" Phase)**
    *   Build a Landing Page for the new model (e.g., "AI-Powered Legal Archive").
    *   Run \$500 in highly targeted LinkedIn/Google ads.
    *   *Output:* Conversion rate data on the new value proposition.
*   **Weeks 5-6: Testing (The "Concierge" Phase)**
    *   Perform the new service *manually* (do not automate yet). If you are pivoting to AI Training Data, manually curate a dataset to see if it sells.
    *   *Output:* Proof of willingness to pay.
*   **Weeks 7-8: Decision (The "Go/No-Go" Phase)**
    *   Compare MVP results against the Pivot Decision Triggers.
    *   Final Board/Stakeholder meeting.

**Stakeholder Communication Template (For 501c3/Foundations):**
> "To ensure the long-term sustainability of our mission to preserve classical knowledge, we are evolving our operational model. While our core mission remains [Mission], we are shifting our technical focus from [Old Model] to [New Model] to increase our capacity for impact and ensure financial independence. This transition will allow us to [Benefit to Foundation]."

### 6. ANTI-PIVOT CHECKLIST
*Do NOT pivot if:*
1.  **Low Sample Size:** You have fewer than 30 transactions in the current model.
2.  **Execution vs. Market:** You are pivoting because the tech is hard (Execution), not because no one wants it (Market).
3.  **Founder Fatigue:** You are pivoting because you are tired of the daily grind, not because the data demands it.
4.  **The "Shiny Object" Fallacy:** You are pivoting toward a "hot" trend (like Generative AI) without a clear way to apply it to your existing assets.
5.  **Misread Unit Economics:** You think you need a pivot, but you actually just need to raise your price from \$5 to \$15.

### 7. CASE STUDIES

1.  **Scale AI:** Started as a general data labeling service for various industries. They pivoted to specialize in "RLHF" (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback) for LLMs, moving from a low-margin commodity to a high-margin AI backbone.
2.  **TranscribeMe:** Originally a general transcription service. They pivoted to provide highly specialized, verified data sets specifically for training voice recognition AI, significantly increasing their value per unit of work.
3.  **Evernote:** Started as a simple "note-taking" tool. They realized the value wasn't the note, but the *organization/search* of the note, pivoting their entire product roadmap toward "Knowledge Management."
