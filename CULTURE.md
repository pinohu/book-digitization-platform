## 1. COMPANY VALUES

### I. Sacred Stewardship
*Preserving the integrity of the past for the benefit of the future.*
* **This means we...** treat every page as a piece of history; double-check OCR accuracy against original texts; prioritize the preservation of meaning over mere speed.
* **This does NOT mean...** being afraid of new technology; being stuck in the past; or neglecting modern efficiency.
* **Interview Question:** "Tell me about a time you were responsible for something of high value or legacy. How did you ensure its integrity was maintained?"
* **Recognition Example:** "The Curator Award" – given to someone who caught a subtle error in the AI's transcription that preserved the original author's intent.

### II. Precision at Scale
*Leveraging AI to achieve perfection in every digit.*
* **This means we...** build systems that are robust; obsess over error rates; seek to automate the mundane so we can focus on the complex.
* **This does NOT mean...** perfectionism that leads to paralysis; ignoring the "good enough" for low-stakes tasks; or assuming the AI is always right.
* **Interview Question:** "How do you balance the need for high-speed output with the necessity of extreme accuracy?"
* **Recognition Example:** "The Algorithm Ace" – celebrating a developer or operator who optimized a workflow to reduce error rates by a significant margin.

### III. Radical Accessibility
*Breaking the barriers between wisdom and the seeker.*
* **This means we...** design for low-bandwidth environments; ensure our platform is intuitive for all users; prioritize making these classics available globally.
* **This does NOT mean...** making the product "cheap" or "low quality"; ignoring the needs of high-end users; or sacrificing security for ease of access.
* **Interview Question:** "How would you design a digital product for someone in a remote area with intermittent internet access?"
* **Recognition Example:** "The Bridge Builder" – recognizing a team member who implemented a feature (like offline mode or lightweight file formats) that expanded our reach.

### IV. Cross-Border Synergy
*Blending US innovation with Nigerian heritage.*
* **This means we...** respect time zone differences; practice extreme empathy for our global colleagues; value the unique perspectives of both our US and Nigerian stakeholders.
* **This does NOT mean...** assuming one region's way of working is superior; working in silos; or ignoring cultural nuances in communication.
* **Interview Question:** "Describe a time you worked on a distributed team. How did you ensure you remained aligned despite distance and cultural differences?"
* **Recognition Example:** "The Global Citizen" – celebrating someone who proactively bridged a communication gap or facilitated seamless collaboration between US and Nigerian teams.

### V. Intellectual Humility
*Learning from the data and each other.*
* **This means we...** admit when our models are wrong; seek feedback aggressively; stay curious about both theology and technology.
* **This does NOT mean...** lacking confidence; being indecisive; or ignoring data in favor of "gut feeling."
* **Interview Question:** "Tell me about a time you were proven wrong by data or a peer. How did you respond?"
* **Recognition Example:** "The Lifelong Learner" – recognizing someone who shared a hard-learned lesson or a new research finding that improved the company.

## 2. CULTURE DOCUMENT

**Our Mission**
To rescue the wisdom of the Catholic Classics from the decay of physical time, transforming them into a digital legacy that is instantly accessible to every student, scholar, and seeker on Earth.

**Our Vision**
A world where the profound intellectual and spiritual heritage of the past is never lost to history, but serves as a living, breathing foundation for the digital future.

**Who We Are**
We are "Technologists with a Soul." We are a hybrid team of AI engineers, historians, and mission-driven operators. We are different because we don't just build software; we build digital vessels for eternal truths. We bridge the gap between the high-tech innovation of the US and the rich, physical heritage of Nigeria.

**How We Work**
We work with the speed of a startup and the reverence of a library. We use asynchronous workflows to respect our global footprint, we value deep work over constant meetings, and we use data to drive our AI, but human wisdom to guide our mission.

**What We Believe About Our Customers**
We believe our users (educators, students, and clergy) are hungry for truth but often limited by geography or resources. They deserve tools that are as reliable as the texts they study.

**What We Believe About Our Craft**
Digitization is not just "scanning." It is a sacred act of translation. Our craft lies in the intersection of machine learning precision and historical accuracy. If the AI fails the text, we have failed our mission.

## 3. DECISION-MAKING PRINCIPLES

**The RAPID Framework**
To avoid "decision by committee," we use:
* **Recommend:** The person who researches and proposes the path.
* **Agree:** Those who must sign off (usually legal or technical leads).
* **Perform:** Those who execute the decision.
* **Input:** Those whose expertise is consulted.
* **Decide:** The single person with the final authority.

**Autonomy vs. Consultation**
* **Decide Alone:** Low-risk, reversible tasks (e.g., adjusting a Slack channel name, minor UI tweaks).
* **Consult:** Medium-risk, high-impact tasks (e.g., changing a software vendor, shifting a sprint goal).
* **Consensus:** High-risk, mission-critical tasks (e.g., changing our core AI architecture, major pivots in mission).

**Type 1 vs. Type 2 Decisions**
* **Type 1 (Irreversible):** "One-way doors." These require deep thought and consultation (e.g., signing a long-term lease, changing the company legal structure).
* **Type 2 (Reversible):** "Two-way doors." These should be made quickly. If they are wrong, we can walk back through them (e.g., testing a new feature, changing a meeting time).

**Disagree and Commit**
Once a decision is made by the "Decider" after hearing all "Input," every team member is expected to support it 100%, even if they initially disagreed. We do not let "I told you so" enter our culture.

**Decision Documentation Template**
* **Context:** What problem are we solving?
* **Options Considered:** What were the alternatives?
* **Decision:** What did we choose?
* **Rationale:** Why this choice over the others?

## 4. COMMUNICATION NORMS

**Async-First Communication**
Because we operate across US and Nigerian time zones, **writing is our default.** We do not schedule a meeting to "give an update" that could have been a written post. Meetings are for debate, complex problem-solving, and human connection.

**Channel Guide**
* **Slack:** Quick questions, social banter, urgent blockers, daily updates.
* **Email:** Formal external communication (partners, Criterion Foundation), official company announcements.
* **Docs (Notion/Google Docs):** The "Source of Truth." Strategy, technical specs, meeting notes, and permanent records.
* **Meetings:** Conflict resolution, brainstorming, 1:1s, and complex technical deep-dives.

**Response Time Expectations**
* **Slack:** Within 4 hours (during your local working hours).
* **Email:** Within 24 hours.
* **Urgent (Phone/Emergency Slack):** Within 1 hour.

**Writing Norms**
* **BLUF:** Bottom Line Up Front. Put your request or main point in the first sentence.
* **Context is King:** Never say "The AI is broken." Say "The OCR engine is failing to recognize Latin characters in the 1920s manuscript series (Link to error log)."
* **Conciseness:** If you can say it in three bullets, don't write three paragraphs.

**The No-Surprise Rule**
Bad news should travel faster than good news. If a project is going to be late or a model is failing, flag it immediately. We fix problems; we don't hide them.

## 5. MEETING CADENCE

* **Daily Standup (15 min):** Async via Slack bot. *What did I do? What am I doing? What are my blockers?*
* **Weekly Team Sync (30 min):** Agenda required in advance. Focus on alignment and high-level blockers.
* **Monthly All-Hands (45 min):** Reviewing metrics (digitization volume, accuracy rates), celebrating wins, and Q&A with leadership.
* **Quarterly Planning (Half-day):** Reviewing OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) and setting the next 90-day roadmap.
* **Annual Offsite (2-3 days):** Strategy, deep team building, and mission reconnection.

**Meeting Hygiene Rules**
1. **No Agenda, No Attenda:** If there is no written agenda, the meeting is cancelled.
2. **Notes are Mandatory:** Every meeting must have a designated note-taker; notes must be published to the shared doc.
3. **Respect the Clock:** Default to 25 or 50-minute meetings to allow for "bio-breaks" between sessions.

## 6. FEEDBACK CULTURE

**Radical Candor**
We practice **Care Personally + Challenge Directly.** We care about your growth and your life, which gives us the right to tell you when your work isn't meeting our standards.

**The SBI Format**
To keep feedback objective, use:
* **Situation:** "During the technical review yesterday..."
* **Behavior:** "...you interrupted the engineer three times before they finished their point."
* **Impact:** "...it made the team hesitant to share ideas and slowed down the troubleshooting."

**Feedback Loops**
* **Regular 1:1s (Weekly, 30 min):** These are *your* meetings. You set the agenda. We discuss growth, blockers, and well-being.
* **Skip-Level 1:1s (Monthly):** For managers to hear from the frontline and for employees to connect with leadership.
* **Anonymous Pulse (Quarterly):** A safe way to share "the truth about the truth" via surveys.
* **Giving Feedback Up:** We encourage you to challenge leadership. If a decision feels wrong or a process is broken, bring it to us with a proposed solution.

## 7. DIVERSITY, EQUITY & INCLUSION (DEI)

**Our Commitment**
BDP is a bridge between cultures. Our mission requires us to honor the diverse voices within the Catholic tradition and the diverse perspectives of our global workforce. We are committed to building a team that reflects the global community we serve.

**Inclusive Practices**
* **Hiring:** We use structured interviews to reduce bias, diverse panels (US and Nigeria) to ensure multi-perspective vetting, and blind resume reviews for initial technical screenings.
* **Pay Equity:** We conduct an annual audit to ensure that compensation is based on role, impact, and experience, regardless of geography or background.
* **Inclusive Language:** We avoid jargon that excludes non-native English speakers and use gender-neutral language in our documentation.
* **Accommodation:** We recognize that "standard" working hours don't work for everyone. We provide flexibility for religious observances, family needs, and neurodivergent working styles.
* **Accountability:** Any instance of discrimination or exclusion will be handled with immediate investigation and zero tolerance for retaliation.
