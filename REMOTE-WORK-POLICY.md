# Remote Work Policy — Book Digitization Platform

This document serves as the official Remote Work Policy for the **Book Digitization Platform**, a joint initiative between **Origin Eyes Incorporated** (USA) and **Criterion Educational Foundation** (Nigeria). 

Our mission is to preserve and digitize Catholic Classics through cutting-edge AI. Because our work spans continents—from the physical archives in Nigeria to the AI development in the United States—this policy is designed to foster deep focus, asynchronous efficiency, and intentional human connection.

---

# Remote Work Policy: Book Digitization Platform

## 1. REMOTE / HYBRID / IN-PERSON DECISION FRAMEWORK
To balance the digital nature of our AI platform with the physical requirements of book digitization, we utilize a tiered role designation system.

### Decision Criteria Matrix
| Role Type | Primary Requirement | Typical Mode | Justification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **AI/Software Dev** | High concentration; Digital output | Fully Remote | Work is entirely cloud-based. |
| **Content/Admin** | Collaboration; Documentation | Hybrid/Remote | Requires syncs but manageable via async. |
| **Digitization Ops** | Physical book handling; Scanning | In-Person | Requires physical access to the archives. |
| **Leadership** | Strategic alignment; Stakeholder mgmt | Hybrid | Mix of deep work and high-touch syncs. |

### Work Mode Definitions
*   **Fully Remote:** No requirement to visit a physical office. Work can be performed from any approved location.
*   **Hybrid:** Requires presence in a designated office/archive for **[X]** days per week (flexible scheduling permitted).
*   **In-Person:** Role requires physical presence at the Criterion archives or specific hardware labs (justified by the need to handle physical Catholic Classics).

### Designation & Relocation
*   **Process:** Role designation is determined by Department Managers and HR. Employees may request a change in mode during quarterly reviews.
*   **Relocation:** Employees must receive written approval before relocating. 
    *   **Tax Notice:** Relocation may trigger new tax nexus requirements for Origin Eyes Inc. or Criterion.
    *   **Cost-of-Living (COLA):** Salary adjustments may be applied if relocation moves an employee to a significantly different economic zone.
*   **International Remote Work:** Due to US/Nigeria operations, international work is permitted but subject to visa compliance and local tax laws. A maximum of **[90]** days per country is recommended to avoid permanent establishment tax issues.

## 2. COMMUNICATION NORMS (ASYNC-FIRST)
We prioritize "writing things down" to ensure our mission survives time zone differences and minimizes "meeting fatigue."

### The Golden Rule: Default to Async
Before scheduling a meeting, ask: *“Can this be resolved via a Notion doc, a Slack thread, or a recorded Loom video?”* If yes, do not schedule a meeting.

### Tool Hierarchy
1.  **Slack:** For quick questions, social interaction, and urgent (but non-emergency) pings.
2.  **Email:** For external partners, formal legal/HR decisions, and long-form external communication.
3.  **Notion/Docs:** The "Single Source of Truth." All specifications, project decisions, and the knowledge base live here.
4.  **Video Calls (Zoom/Google Meet):** Reserved for brainstorming, complex conflict resolution, and relationship building.

### Response Time Expectations
*   **Slack:** Within **4 business hours**.
*   **Email:** Within **24 business hours**.
*   **@Urgent Tag:** Within **1 hour** (only to be used for true blockers).
*   **After Hours:** There is **zero expectation** of response outside of an employee's local business hours.

### Communication Etiquette
*   **Over-communication:** Share the "why" behind your decisions. Document the context so a teammate in a different time zone can pick up where you left off.
*   **Status Indicators:** Always use Slack statuses to signal availability (e.g., 🎧 Focus Time, 🏃‍♂️ OOO, 🗓️ In a Meeting).

## 3. MEETING POLICY
Meetings are an investment of company time; we must ensure a high Return on Investment (ROI).

*   **The Cap:** No individual should spend more than **15 hours per week** in internal meetings.
*   **No-Meeting Days:** **Tuesday and Thursday** are company-wide "Focus Days." No internal meetings are to be scheduled.
*   **Meeting Hygiene:**
    *   **Agenda Required:** No agenda = No meeting. Agendas must be shared **24 hours in advance**.
    *   **The 25/50 Rule:** Meetings default to **25 or 50 minutes** (not 30/60) to allow for bio-breaks and transition time.
    *   **Documentation:** Minutes and action items must be posted to Notion within **24 hours**.
    *   **Optionality:** Attendees marked "Optional" are encouraged to decline to protect their focus time.
    *   **Monthly Audit:** All recurring meetings are reviewed monthly; if they lack clear value, they are deleted.
*   **Camera Policy:** Cameras **ON** for 1:1s and small groups to build rapport. Cameras **OPTIONAL** for large All-Hands meetings.
*   **Meeting-Free Weeks:** One week per quarter is designated as a "Meeting-Free Week" to allow for deep-dive project sprints.

## 4. TIME ZONE MANAGEMENT
We bridge the gap between US and Nigerian operations through disciplined scheduling.

*   **Core Collaboration Hours:** **10:00 AM – 2:00 PM ET**. All synchronous meetings must occur within this window.
*   **Time Zone Map:** A live team map is maintained in Notion to visualize overlap.
*   **Calendar Etiquette:** Employees must display their working hours in their digital calendars and block personal/family time.
*   **Handoff Practices:** To ensure the "sun never sets" on our digitization, teams use **Async Standups** (written updates in Slack) to pass tasks from one time zone to the next.
*   **Fairness:** If a meeting must occur outside core hours, the "inconvenience" must be rotated so the same region isn't always meeting late at night.

## 5. HOME OFFICE STIPEND
To ensure our team can work effectively, we invest in their physical environment.

*   **One-Time Setup Stipend:** **$1,000 USD** for ergonomic desks, chairs, monitors, and peripherals.
*   **Monthly Utility Stipend:** **$100 USD/month** to offset high-speed internet and electricity costs.
*   **Reimbursement:** Submit receipts via the expense portal; approval is processed within **1 week**.
*   **Ownership:** Equipment purchased with company funds remains company property. If an employee leaves within **1 year**, the cost of the equipment will be deducted from the final payout.
*   **Refresh Cycle:** An additional **$500 USD annually** is available for hardware upgrades or replacements.

---

## 6. CO-WORKING BUDGET
For those who find home environments distracting, we support professional third-party spaces.

*   **Monthly Co-working Allowance:** **$200 USD/month** (e.g., WeWork or local Nigerian co-working hubs).
*   **Team Co-working:** Team Leads are encouraged to coordinate "Co-working Days" where the local team meets in a shared space, fully funded by the company.
*   **Cafe Allowance:** A **$50 USD/month** stipend is provided for coffee/food when working from local cafes.

---

## 7. IN-PERSON GATHERING CADENCE
Digital work requires periodic physical connection to maintain culture and mission alignment.

*   **Quarterly Team Gatherings:** 2–3 day regional meetups (company-funded travel + lodging).
*   **Annual Company Offsite:** 3–5 day intensive strategy and team-building retreat.
*   **Budgeting:** **$2,000–$3,000 per person** (inclusive of airfare, hotel, activities, and meals).
*   **Planning:** 8 weeks lead time for quarterly; 12 weeks for annual offsites.
*   **The 50/30/20 Format:** 
    *   **50%** Focused Work/Strategy Sessions.
    *   **30%** Structured Team Building.
    *   **20%** Unstructured Social Time.
*   **Virtual Inclusion:** If travel is impossible for an employee, they will be provided with high-quality audio/visual access to participate virtually in all sessions.

---

## 8. WELLNESS & BOUNDARIES
Our mission is long-term; we prevent burnout by respecting human limits.

*   **Right to Disconnect:** Employees are strictly prohibited from being penalized for not responding to communications outside their local working hours.
*   **Flexible Hours:** Within the Core Collaboration Window, employees have autonomy over their start/end times.
*   **Mental Health Days:** **2 days per quarter** are provided as "No-Questions-Asked" mental health days.
*   **Annual Wellness Stipend:** **$500 USD/year** for gym memberships, meditation apps (Calm/Headspace), or therapy co-pays.
*   **Burnout Prevention:** Managers are trained to monitor workload via Notion/Slack activity. Mandatory PTO minimums are enforced to ensure everyone takes time to rest and recharge.
