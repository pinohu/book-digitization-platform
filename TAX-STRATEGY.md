# TAX STRATEGY GUIDE: AI Book Digitization Platform

**Focus:** Scaling an AI-driven transactional SaaS/Service model.

### 1. R&D TAX CREDITS (The "Innovation" Offset)
Since your platform uses AI for document digitization, you are not just a "service" business; you are a "technology development" business. This is the most undervalued asset for early-stage tech startups.

*   **Qualification Criteria:** You must satisfy the "Four-Part Test": (1) Permissible purpose (improving OCR accuracy/speed), (2) Elimination of uncertainty (how to handle old/damaged Catholic texts), (3) Process of experimentation, and (4) Technological in nature.
*   **Qualifying Activities:**
    *   **Software Development:** Coding the AI engine and API integrations.
    *   **Algorithm Design:** Improving machine learning models to recognize archaic fonts/layouts.
    *   **UX Research:** Designing the workflow for users to upload and verify digitized books.
    *   **Cloud Architecture:** Engineering the scalability of the processing pipeline.
*   **Potential Credit Calculation:**
    *   If you spend **$200,000** on qualified wages (developers) and contract research:
    *   **Federal Credit:** ~$13,000–$20,000 (depending on the specific percentage of qualifying vs. non-qualifying time).
*   **State R&D Credits (Top 5 Startup States):** California, New York, Texas, Massachusetts, Illinois. (Note: Some states offer credits that can be carried forward indefinitely).
*   **Documentation Requirements:** **Crucial.** You cannot "estimate" at year-end. You need:
    *   *Contemporaneous Logs:* Jira tickets, GitHub commits, and Trello boards.
    *   *Time Allocation:* Employee timesheets specifically marking "R&D" vs. "Maintenance/Support."
*   **Payroll Tax Offset:** For startups with <$5M in revenue, the IRS allows you to apply the credit against the **employer portion of Social Security/Medicare taxes** (up to $250k/year) rather than just income tax. This is vital when you have zero profit but high R&D spend.

### 2. QSBS SECTION 1202 (The "Exit" Multiplier)
If you intend to sell this platform in 5–10 years, this is your most important structural decision.

*   **The Benefit:** You may be able to exclude **100% of capital gains** (up to $10M or 10x your basis) from federal taxes upon the sale of the company.
*   **Qualification Requirements:**
    *   **Entity Type:** Must be a **Domestic C-Corp**. (LLCs and S-Corps do not qualify).
    *   **Original Issuance:** You must acquire the stock directly from the company in exchange for money or property.
    *   **Holding Period:** Must hold the stock for at least **5 years**.
    *   **Active Business Test:** The company must use at least 80% of its assets in the "active conduct" of a qualified trade. 
    *   **Assessment:** An **AI Platform for Document Digitization** is a "product-based" technology business, which qualifies. It is *not* a professional service (like a law firm) or a highly liquid asset (like a bank).
*   **Planning Strategy:** Ensure all early-stage funding and founder equity are issued as C-Corp stock from day one.
*   **State Warning:** **California does not conform to QSBS.** While you might pay $0 in Federal tax on a $10M sale, CA will still want its slice.

### 3. STATE TAX OPTIMIZATION (The "Nexus" Strategy)
*   **Incorporation Location:**
    *   **Delaware (DE):** The gold standard for VC-backed startups. High franchise taxes if you scale, but best for legal precedent and investor comfort.
    *   **Wyoming (WY) / Nevada (NV):** Zero state income tax. Excellent for "lean" bootstrapped startups.
*   **Nexus Rules:** Even if you incorporate in WY, if you have an employee in Nigeria (via a local entity) or a developer in California, you create "Nexus" in those locations. You will owe taxes where the *activity* occurs.
*   **Sales Tax (SaaS/Transactional):** Since you charge $5/book, you are a transactional business.
    *   **SaaS Taxability:** Many states tax "Software as a Service." You must monitor "Economic Nexus" thresholds (e.g., if you sell $100k+ into Texas, you must collect Texas sales tax). Use tools like **Avalara** or **TaxJar**.

### 4. ENTITY-SPECIFIC STRATEGIES (The "Growth" Roadmap)

| Revenue Stage | Recommended Entity | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **<$100K** | **LLC (Single Member)** | Simplest. All profit/loss flows to your personal return. Minimal compliance. |
| **$100K–$500K** | **S-Corp (via LLC)** | Save on FICA. Pay yourself a "reasonable salary" and take the rest as a distribution (not subject to 15.3% self-employment tax). |
| **$500K–$1M** | **C-Corp** | If you are seeking VC funding or want to utilize R&D payroll offsets and QSBS. |
| **$1M+** | **C-Corp** | Full focus on scaling, institutional investment, and tax-efficient retained earnings. |

**Strategic Note on the 501(c)(3):** To protect the nonprofit status of *Origin Eyes Inc.*, the Digitization Platform should be a **separate for-profit C-Corp**. The nonprofit can partner with the for-profit, but the for-profit cannot be "run" by the nonprofit without triggering **UBIT (Unrelated Business Income Tax)** and risking the nonprofit's tax-exempt status.

### 5. ESTIMATED QUARTERLY TAXES (The "Survival" Plan)
The IRS does not wait until April to collect.
*   **When to start:** As soon as you expect to owe **>$1,000** in taxes for the year.
*   **Safe Harbor Rules:** To avoid underpayment penalties, pay either:
    1.  **100%** of the tax shown on your *prior year's* return, OR
    2.  **90%** of the tax shown on your *current year's* return.
*   **Schedule:** April 15, June 15, Sept 15, Jan 15.

### 6. DEDUCTION MAXIMIZATION (The "Cash Flow" Play)
*   **Startup Costs:** You can deduct up to **$5,000** in the first year of business; the remainder must be amortized over **180 months**.
*   **Section 179:** If you buy high-end servers or specialized scanning hardware, you can "expense" the full cost in Year 1 rather than depreciating it over years.
*   **Home Office:** If you are running the platform from a dedicated space, deduct a percentage of rent/utilities.
*   **Retirement:** As the owner, use a **Solo 401(k)**. It allows for much higher contribution limits than a standard IRA, reducing your taxable income significantly.

### 7. ROBS (ROLLOVER AS BUSINESS STARTUP)
*   **The Concept:** A way to use your existing 401(k)/IRA funds to fund your new C-Corp without paying early withdrawal penalties.
*   **The Mechanics:**
    1.  Establish a new **C-Corp**.
    2.  Establish a new **Qualified Retirement Plan** for that C-Corp.
    3.  Roll over your old retirement funds into the new plan.
    4.  The new plan purchases stock in your C-Corp.
*   **The "Why":** It provides immediate working capital without the 10% penalty or immediate income tax hit.
*   **The Danger (Mandatory Warning):** ROBS is highly scrutinized by the IRS. 
    *   **Prohibited Transactions:** You cannot use the money to pay yourself a salary that isn't "reasonable," nor can you use it to pay personal debts.
    *   **Compliance:** You **must** use a ROBS-compliant Third-Party Administrator (TPA). 
    *   **Exit Strategy:** When you sell the company, the retirement plan owns the proceeds, not you personally (unless you take a distribution).
*   **Verdict:** Use ROBS only if you have significant retirement capital and a clear C-Corp roadmap. **Do not attempt this via an LLC.**

### 8. WHEN TO HIRE A CPA (The "Complexity" Trigger)
*   **Revenue Thresholds:**
    *   **$0–$100k:** Use DIY tools (**Bench, Pilot, or Fondo**) to keep books clean.
    *   **$100k–$500k:** Hire a fractional CPA to manage quarterly filings and S-Corp elections.
    *   **$500k+ or International Nexus:** Hire a full-service firm.
*   **Complexity Triggers:**
    *   You start hiring employees (W-2).
    *   You begin transferring funds between the US and Nigeria.
    *   You want to claim R&D credits.
*   **What to look for:** A "Startup CPA" who understands **SaaS metrics** and **Section 1202**. Do not hire a generalist who only does personal tax returns.
*   **Questions to Ask:**
    1.  "Have you worked with R&D tax credit claims for software companies?"
    2.  "Are you familiar with the tax implications of US-Nigeria cross-border payments?"
    3.  "Do you understand the compliance requirements for a ROBS structure?"
