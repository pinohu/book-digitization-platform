*   *Why it matters:* If your Gross Burn is $10k but you make $8k in revenue, your Net Burn is only $2k. You will last much longer than the Gross Burn suggests.

### 3. Default Alive vs. Default Dead
*Framework by Paul Graham (Y Combinator)*

To determine your status, look at your **Revenue Growth Rate** vs. your **Expense Growth Rate**.

**The Calculation:**
1.  Calculate your current monthly revenue growth (e.g., +10% per month).
2.  Project that growth forward against your fixed/variable costs.
3.  **Default Alive:** If your revenue growth curve intersects your expense curve *before* your cash reaches zero, you are "Default Alive." You don't *need* more money to survive; you only need it to grow faster.
4.  **Default Dead:** If your revenue growth is too slow to cover your burn, you will hit $0 before becoming profitable. You *must* raise capital or change the model to survive.

### 7. Break-Even Analysis
This tells you exactly how many books you must digitize to stop losing money.

**Assumptions:**
*   **Price per Book (P):** $5.00
*   **Variable Cost per Book (V):** $1.50 (AI API costs + storage + human verification)
*   **Contribution Margin (CM):** $P - V = \mathbf{\$3.50}$
*   **Total Monthly Fixed Costs (FC):** (e.g., $8,000/mo for salaries/rent/software)

**The Break-Even Formula:**
$$\text{Break-even Volume (Books)} = \frac{\text{Fixed Costs}}{\text{Contribution Margin}}$$

**Calculation:**
$$\text{Break-even Volume} = \frac{\$8,000}{\$3.50} = \mathbf{2,286 \text{ books per month}}$$

**Strategic Insight:** To be "Default Alive," Origin Eyes Inc. must ensure it can scale the sales pipeline to exceed **2,286 books every month** before the initial cash reserves are exhausted.
