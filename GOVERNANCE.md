# Governance Framework — Book Digitization Platform

This governance framework is designed for **Book Digitization Platform**, an AI-driven startup. As the company moves from a research-heavy pre-seed stage to a scaling Series B company, the governance must transition from "Founder-led/Informal" to "Institutional/Structured."

---

### 1. Board Composition Evolution
The board grows in complexity as more capital is raised and more stakeholders (investors) require oversight.

| Stage | Total Seats | Composition | Focus |
| :--- | :---: | :--- | :--- |
| **Pre-Seed** | 1–2 | 100% Founders | Product development & IP creation. |
| **Seed** | 3 | 2 Founders, 1 Lead Investor | Product-Market Fit & early hiring. |
| **Series A** | 5 | 2 Founders, 2 Investors, 1 Independent | Scaling operations & unit economics. |
| **Series B** | 7 | 2 Founders, 3 Investors, 2 Independents | Institutionalization & international expansion. |

*   **Founders:** Represent the vision and technical roadmap.
*   **Investors:** Represent the capital and fiduciary oversight for shareholders.
*   **Independents:** Usually industry veterans (e.g., former publishing execs or AI scale-up experts) to provide unbiased judgment and mitigate conflicts between founders and investors.

---

### 2. Fiduciary Duties (Explained Plainly)
Board members are legally obligated to act in the best interest of the company, not themselves or their specific fund.

1.  **Duty of Care:** "Do your homework." You must make decisions after being fully informed. You cannot simply "rubber stamp" CEO requests; you must ask hard questions about AI accuracy, burn rates, and IP risks.
2.  **Duty of Loyalty:** "Company first." You must put the company’s interests above your own. If you have a side business that competes with the digitization platform, you must disclose it and recuse yourself from votes.
3.  **Duty of Good Faith:** "Act honestly." You must act with an honest belief that your actions are in the best interest of the company.

### 3. Meeting Cadence & Agenda Templates

#### **Cadence**
*   **Pre-Seed/Seed:** Monthly "Update Meetings" (informal) + Quarterly Board Meetings (formal).
*   **Series A/B:** Monthly Management Meetings + Quarterly Formal Board Meetings + Annual Strategy Offsites.

#### **Standard Board Agenda Template**
1.  **Consent Agenda (5 mins):** Approval of previous minutes.
2.  **CEO Report (20 mins):** High-level vision, major wins, and "The Big Problem" currently facing the company.
3.  **Financial Review (15 mins):** Burn rate, runway, revenue (SaaS/API usage), and budget vs. actuals.
4.  **KPI Deep Dive (30 mins):** *Specific to Book Digitization:* OCR accuracy rates, throughput (pages/hour), customer acquisition cost (CAC), and compute/GPU costs.
5.  **Strategic Topic (45 mins):** (e.g., "Reviewing the M&A target" or "AI Ethics/IP Compliance Framework").
6.  **Executive Session (15 mins):** Independent/Investor members only (no Founders) to discuss CEO performance.

---

### 4. Committee Structure
As the company reaches Series A/B, the board should delegate heavy lifting to committees.

*   **Audit Committee:** Oversees financial reporting, internal controls, and legal/regulatory compliance (crucial for AI data privacy/copyright issues).
*   **Compensation Committee:** Sets CEO/Executive pay and manages the Employee Stock Option Pool (ESOP).
*   **Nominating & Governance Committee:** Identifies new board members and ensures the board is following its own rules.

---

### 5. Information Rights & Reporting Packages
Investors require a "Reporting Package" (usually sent 48-72 hours before a meeting) to ensure informed decision-making.

**The Monthly/Quarterly Package should include:**
*   **Financials:** P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow Statement.
*   **SaaS Metrics:** MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue), Churn, Net Revenue Retention.
*   **Operational Metrics:** Model accuracy benchmarks, GPU utilization, and data ingestion volume.
*   **Legal/HR:** Headcount growth, key hires/departures, and any pending IP litigation.

**Observer Rights:**
Commonly granted to "minority" investors who do not have a voting seat. They may attend meetings and receive reports but **cannot vote** on board matters.

---

### 6. Consent Matters (Board Approval Required)
The following "Reserved Matters" cannot be decided by the CEO alone; they require a formal Board vote:
*   Issuance of new equity or significant debt.
*   Sale of the company or major assets (IP).
*   Changes to the Company Charter or Bylaws.
*   Hiring/Firing of the CEO or C-suite.
*   Annual Budget approval.
*   Entering into transactions with "Related Parties" (e.g., the company renting an office from the Founder).

---

### 7. Advisory Board Structure
The Advisory Board provides specialized expertise without the legal liability of the Board of Directors.

*   **Composition:** Experts in Large Language Models (LLMs), Copyright Law, or Global Publishing Distribution.
*   **Compensation:** **0.1% to 0.5% equity**, typically vesting over 2 years.
*   **Role:** They provide "informal" guidance and introductions but have no voting power or fiduciary duty to the company.

---

### 8. D&O Insurance (Directors & Officers)
**Requirement:** As soon as the company accepts outside institutional capital (Seed/Series A), it **must** purchase D&O Insurance.

*   **Purpose:** Protects the personal assets of directors and officers if they are sued for "breach of fiduciary duty" or mismanagement.
*   **Why it matters:** High-quality independent directors will refuse to join a board if they are not covered by a robust D&O policy.

---

### 9. Governance Evolution Timeline

| Milestone | Governance Maturity | Key Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pre-Seed** | **Founder-Centric** | Formalize Incorporation & IP Assignment. |
| **Seed** | **Investor-Aligned** | Grant Observer Rights; implement monthly reporting. |
| **Series A** | **Institutionalizing** | Form Compensation Committee; hire first Independent Director. |
| **Series B** | **Regulated/Structured** | Form Audit Committee; implement full D&O; formalize Board evaluations. |
