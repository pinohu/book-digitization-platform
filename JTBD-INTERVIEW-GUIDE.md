# Jobs-to-be-Done Interview Guide — Book Digitization Platform

This guide is designed to help you move beyond "user preferences" and uncover the underlying **motivations** driving customers to adopt or reject an AI-powered Book Digitization Platform.

---

# 1. JTBD Theory Overview
In JTBD, we don't buy products; we "hire" them to do a job. A "Job" is not a task, but a transformation.

*   **Functional Jobs:** The core task. *Example: "Convert a 500-page scanned PDF into a searchable, structured JSON format."*
*   **Emotional Jobs:** How the user wants to feel. *Example: "Feel confident that no critical data was lost during the conversion."*
*   **Social Jobs:** How the user wants to be perceived. *Example: "Look like a cutting-edge, efficient researcher to my department head."*

---

# 2. Recruitment Criteria
To get valid data, you must interview people who have recently "switched" or are in the process of switching.

*   **Target Personas:** Archivists, Academic Researchers, Legal Document Managers, Library Technologists, or Content Digitization Project Managers.
*   **Inclusion Criteria:** 
    *   Must have experienced a "struggling moment" with document accessibility in the last 6 months.
    *   Must have either implemented a new digitization tool OR be actively evaluating AI solutions.
*   **Exclusion Criteria:** People who have no interest in digitization or those who have never felt "pain" from their current workflow.

---

# 3. The Switch Interview Framework (Timeline)
We map the user's journey through the "Switch" to find where the friction lies.

1.  **First Thought:** The moment the current way of working became unacceptable.
2.  **Passive Looking:** Browsing articles, hearing about AI, "what if" thinking.
3.  **Active Looking:** Comparing specific AI platforms, requesting demos, reading reviews.
4.  **Deciding:** The specific moment of choosing one platform over another (or staying with the old way).
5.  **Consuming:** The first time they used the tool to digitize a real book/document.
6.  **Satisfaction:** The realization of whether the "job" was actually completed.

---

# 4. The 20 Interview Questions

### A. The Struggling Moment (Identifying the Trigger)
1. "Tell me about the last time you realized your current way of handling documents wasn't working."
2. "What specifically happened in that moment that made you say, 'We need a different way'?"
3. "How much time/money was being lost because of that specific problem?"
4. "What were you trying to achieve when the frustration peaked?"

### B. The Push (Pain of the Current Situation)
5. "What is the most tedious part of your current digitization workflow?"
6. "If you kept doing things the way you are now, what would the consequence be in a year?"
7. "How much do you rely on manual human intervention right now?"
8. "What is the biggest risk associated with your current method?"

### C. The Pull (Attraction of the New AI Solution)
9. "When you first heard about AI-driven digitization, what was the first thing that came to mind?"
10. "What specific capability did you hope an AI platform would provide that your current tool lacks?"
11. "How did you imagine your workday would change if this 'job' was automated?"
12. "What was the 'magic moment' you were looking for in a demo?"

### D. The Anxiety (Fear of the New Solution)
13. "What were you worried might go wrong when you first considered switching to an AI platform?"
14. "What was your biggest concern regarding the accuracy of the AI-extracted text?"
15. "How did you feel about the possibility of your team having to learn a completely new system?"
16. "What would have been a 'deal-breaker' during your evaluation process?"

### E. The Habit (The Weight of the Old Way)
17. "Before you looked for a new solution, how long had you been using your current method?"
18. "What made it difficult to simply stick with what you already knew?"
19. "Were there any 'workarounds' you had created that you were actually quite fond of?"
20. "What was the one thing about your old process that you were actually afraid of losing?"

# 5. Frameworks for Analysis

### Forces of Progress Diagram
Use this to visualize why users move.
*   **Push:** The pain of the current manual/old process.
*   **Pull:** The promise of AI speed/accuracy.
*   **Anxiety:** Fear of AI hallucinations, data security, or cost.
*   **Habit/Inertia:** "We've always done it this way" or "The current tool is 'good enough'."

### Timeline Mapping Template
| Phase | User Action/Thought | Key Emotion | Friction/Enabler |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **First Thought** | *Realized OCR was failing* | *Frustration* | *Push: High error rate* |
| **Active Looking** | *Googled 'AI Document Extraction'* | *Curiosity* | *Pull: Speed promise* |
| **Deciding** | *Chose Platform X* | *Nervousness* | *Anxiety: Security concerns* |

### Synthesis Framework: Identifying Jobs
Group your interview notes into three buckets:
1.  **The "Why":** The core struggle (e.g., "Data is trapped in unsearchable images").
2.  **The "How":** The context (e.g., "During high-volume archival projects").
3.  **The "Success":** The desired outcome (e.g., "A searchable database with 99% accuracy").

# 6. Translating Findings into Product Features

| If the User says... | The Job is... | Product Feature to Build |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| *"I'm terrified the AI will hallucinate facts."* | **Emotional Job:** Ensure data integrity. | **Confidence Scores:** Show a percentage of how certain the AI is about each word. |
| *"I spend all day fixing typos from the scan."* | **Functional Job:** Reduce manual correction. | **Smart Auto-Correction:** AI that learns from user manual edits. |
| *"My boss wants these archives online by next month."* | **Social Job:** Meet high-pressure deadlines. | **Batch Processing/Bulk Upload:** Ability to ingest 100 books at once. |

# 7. Example Job Statements

1.  **When I** am managing a massive archive of historical manuscripts, **I want to** use AI to automatically extract and structure metadata, **so I can** make the collection searchable for researchers without hiring a team of transcribers.
2.  **When I** encounter a low-quality, faded scan, **I want to** use advanced image enhancement, **so I can** ensure the extracted text is accurate enough for legal compliance.
3.  **When I** am transitioning from manual data entry to a digital system, **I want to** have a seamless bulk-import process, **so I can** avoid the anxiety of losing data during the migration.
4.  **When I** present my progress to my stakeholders, **I want to** show high-speed digitization metrics, **so I can** prove the ROI of our new technology investment.
5.  **When I** am working with sensitive, confidential documents, **I want to** ensure the AI processing happens in a secure, private environment, **so I can** maintain client trust and data privacy.
