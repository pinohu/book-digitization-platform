# Customer Discovery Script — Book Digitization Platform

> Interview guide for your first 10 customers
> Generated: April 20, 2026

This script is designed based on **The Mom Test** philosophy: avoid asking "Would you use this?" (which leads to false positives) and instead focus on the user's **past behavior and current pain.**

---

## ## Interview Setup

**Who to Target (The "Partnership" Personas):**
To validate PMF, you need to talk to the *decision-maker* who feels the pain of physical archives.
1.  **University/Public Librarians:** Head of Digital Collections or Archivists.
2.  **Legal Firm Partners:** Managing partners at firms with massive legacy case files.
3.  **Museum Curators:** Heads of archives or historical societies.
4.  **Private Collection Managers:** People managing estates for wealthy collectors.

**How to Recruit:**
*   **LinkedIn Outreach:** "I'm researching the bottlenecks in document digitization for [Industry]. I'm not selling anything; I just want to understand how you currently handle your archives. Do you have 20 minutes?"
*   **Cold Email:** Focus on the *burden* of physical storage and the *risk* of degradation.
*   **Warm Intros:** Ask your network for anyone who manages "legacy paper records."

**Logistics:**
*   **Tooling:** Zoom/Google Meet + Otter.ai or Grain (for transcription).
*   **Mindset:** You are a detective, not a salesperson. If they say "I love the idea," ignore it. If they say "I spent 40 hours last month manually typing this," write it in bold.

## ## Interview Script (20 Minutes)

### Warm-up (2 min)
*Goal: Establish rapport and understand their scale.*
1. "To start, could you describe your current role and how much of your time is spent managing physical books or documents?"
2. "Roughly how many physical volumes/pages are currently in your archives that *should* be digital but aren't?"

### Problem Discovery (8 min)
*Goal: Find "bleeding neck" problems. Focus on the PAST.*
1. **"Walk me through the last time you tried to digitize a book or a set of documents. How did you do it?"**
    *   *Probe:* Did you use a scanner? A third-party service? A manual intern?
2. **"What was the most frustrating part of that process?"**
    *   *Probe:* Was it the speed? The OCR accuracy? The cost? The physical handling of the book?
3. **"How much time or money did that specific project cost you?"**
    *   *Probe:* If they don't know, ask: "If you had to guess, how many man-hours went into it?"
4. **"Have you tried any other tools or services to solve this in the past? Why didn't they work out?"**
    *   *Probe:* Look for gaps in current market leaders (e.g., "Too expensive," "Too slow," "Ruined the pages").
5. **"What happens if these books stay in physical form for another 5 years?"**
    *   *Probe:* Is there a risk of loss? Lack of access? Compliance issues? (This validates the *urgency*).

### Solution Validation (5 min)
*Goal: Test the value proposition and price point without "selling."*
1. **The Pitch:** "If there was an AI-powered platform that could digitize books with [mention key feature, e.g., 99% OCR accuracy/automatic formatting] for $5 per book, how would that change your current workflow?"
2. **The "Must-Have" Question:** "If you were to use a service like this, what is the one thing it *must* do perfectly for you to trust it with your archives?"
3. **Price Sensitivity:** "At $5 per book, does that feel like a bargain, a fair price, or expensive compared to what you're doing now?"

### Closing (5 min)
*Goal: Expand the pipeline and secure a commitment.*
1. **The Referral:** "Who else in your network is struggling with the burden of physical archives that I should talk to?"
2. **The Commitment:** "I'm building a beta version of this. If I can solve [the specific pain they mentioned], would you be open to being one of my first 5 pilot users?"
3. **Thanks:** "Thank you for your time. Your insights are incredibly helpful."

## ## Scoring Rubric (Signal Analysis)

After each interview, score the lead from 1–5 on these signals.

| Signal | Weak PMF (1-2) | Strong PMF (4-5) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pain Intensity** | "It's a bit annoying/slow." | "It's a nightmare; it's our biggest bottleneck." |
| **Existing Spend** | No budget; they just ignore the problem. | Already paying for expensive, low-quality services. |
| **Urgency** | "Maybe we'll look at this next year." | "I need this solved by the end of the quarter." |
| **Price Perception** | "$5 is too high for a digital file." | "$5 is a steal compared to manual labor costs." |
| **Commitment** | "Sure, send me an email when it's done." | "Can I sign up for the beta today? When can we start?" |

## ## After 10 Interviews: The Decision Framework

Once you have 10 completed scripts, aggregate the scores.

### 🟢 PROCEED (The Green Light)
*   **Criteria:** 7+ interviews scored 4s and 5s across Pain and Price.
*   **Action:** Build the MVP. Focus exclusively on the "Must-Have" feature identified in the solution validation phase. Stop interviewing and start shipping.

### 🟡 PIVOT (The Yellow Light)
*   **Criteria:** People have the problem (High Pain), but they hate the price or the delivery method (Low Price/Solution score).
*   **Action:** Adjust the model. 
    *   *Example:* Maybe they don't want to pay $5/book, but they would pay $1,000/month for an enterprise license. 
    *   *Example:* Maybe they don't want a "platform," they want a "managed service" where you handle the shipping.

### 🔴 KILL (The Red Light)
*   **Criteria:** Most interviews were "polite." Low pain, no existing spend, and no urgency.
*   **Action:** Stop. The problem isn't "painful" enough for people to pay for. Search for a different target persona (e.g., move from Public Libraries $\rightarrow$ High-end Law Firms) or a different problem entirely.
