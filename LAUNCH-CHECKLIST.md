# Launch Checklist — Book Digitization Platform

This is a specialized launch. Because your primary use case involves **Criterion Educational Foundation** (preserving sacred/classic texts) and your structure involves a **US 501c3**, your launch isn't just about "software users"—it is about **authority, accuracy, and preservation.**

A generic Product Hunt launch might get you developers, but a "Case Study Launch" will get you publishers and institutions.

Here is your comprehensive, tailored launch checklist.

---

### **PHASE 1: PRE-LAUNCH (T-30 to T-1)**
*Goal: Transition from "AI Tool" to "Proven Preservation Solution."*

**T-30 to T-21: Foundation & Proof of Concept**
- [ ] **The "Criterion Pilot":** Complete the digitization of a small subset of Criterion’s Catholic Classics. This is your "Golden Sample."
- [ ] **Finalize MVP:** Ensure the AI handles old fonts, religious terminology, and non-standard layouts common in older books.
- [ ] **Set up Analytics:** Track "Upload-to-Completion" conversion funnels (crucial for a transactional model).
- [ ] **Landing Page:** Create a page that speaks to two audiences: *Publishers* (efficiency) and *Archives/Foundations* (preservation).
- [ ] **Waitlist & Lead Magnet:** Offer a "Free Sample Digitization" for the first 50 signups to build the list.
- [ ] **Draft Press Kit:** Include the "Origin Eyes + Criterion" story (The mission of preserving culture through AI).
- [ ] **Targeted Media List:** Identify 20 journalists in *EdTech*, 20 in *Religious/Catholic Media*, and 10 in *AI/Tech*.
- [ ] **Social Setup:** Focus LinkedIn (B2B/Foundations) and X/Twitter (AI/Tech).

**T-20 to T-11: Content & Social Proof**
- [ ] **The "Before & After" Video:** A high-quality 60-second video showing a physical Criterion book being scanned and turning into a perfect, searchable digital format.
- [ ] **Case Study Draft:** Write a short document: *"How Criterion Educational Foundation is digitizing Catholic Classics using [Platform Name]."*
- [ ] **Email Sequences:** 
    - *The Vision:* Why digitization matters for culture.
    - *The Tech:* How our AI handles complex documents.
    - *The Offer:* $5/book introductory pricing.
- [ ] **Customer Support Setup:** Create a "Quality Guarantee" FAQ (How do you handle errors in sacred texts?).
- [ ] **Onboarding Flow:** Ensure the path from "Upload" $\rightarrow$ "Pay $5" $\rightarrow$ "Download" is frictionless.

**T-10 to T-1: Technical Hardening**
- [ ] **QA Pass:** Specifically test "Edge Case" documents (stained pages, faded ink, margins).
- [ ] **Load Testing:** Ensure the server can handle multiple large PDF uploads simultaneously.
- [ ] **Payment Gateway Test:** Verify $5 transactions work seamlessly in both US (Stripe) and potentially African (Flutterwave/Paystack) contexts if needed.
- [ ] **Launch Assets:** Prepare the "Launch Day" social media kit.
- [ ] **Real-time Monitoring:** Set up alerts for "Failed Uploads" or "Payment Errors."

**🎯 PRE-LAUNCH TARGET METRICS:**
*   **Waitlist Size:** 100–500 highly targeted leads.
*   **Pilot Success:** 98%+ accuracy on the Criterion sample set.
*   **Landing Page Conversion:** >10% (Visitor to Email Capture).

---

### **PHASE 2: LAUNCH DAY (Hour-by-Hour)**
*Goal: Maximum visibility and immediate transaction validation.*

- **00:01 — Product Hunt Launch:** Position as "The AI tool for preserving history/documents."
- **06:00 — The "Mission" Email:** Send to waitlist. Subject: *Preserving the classics: [Platform Name] is live.*
- **07:00 — The "Visual" Post (X/LinkedIn):** Post the "Before & After" video of the Criterion books.
- **09:00 — Community Outreach:** Post in Reddit (r/archiving, r/publishing, r/AI) and Indie Hackers.
- **10:00 — Engagement:** Respond to every Product Hunt comment.
- **12:00 — The "Social Proof" Push:** Post a screenshot of the first successful $5 transaction or a testimonial from Criterion.
- **14:00 — Direct Outreach:** Send personalized emails to the "Targeted Media List" with the Criterion Case Study attached.
- **16:00 — LinkedIn Deep Dive:** Long-form post on the intersection of 501c3 mission and AI technology.
- **18:00 — Milestone Update:** "50 books digitized in 12 hours!"
- **22:00 — Debrief:** Analyze which channel brought the first $5 user.

**🎯 LAUNCH DAY TARGET METRICS:**
*   **Traffic:** 500+ unique visitors.
*   **Conversion:** 1–3% of visitors completing a $5 transaction.
*   **Engagement:** 20+ comments/inquiries on social platforms.

---

### **PHASE 3: POST-LAUNCH (Week 1)**
*Goal: Stabilization and scaling the B2B pipeline.*

**Day 1-2: The "Cleanup"**
- [ ] **Bug Squashing:** Fix any errors found in the first 50 uploads.
- [ ] **Direct Follow-up:** Email everyone who signed up but *didn't* upload a book. Ask: "What stopped you?"
- [ ] **Metric Review:** Calculate "Cost Per Acquisition" (CPA) vs. the $5 revenue.

**Day 3-5: The "Expansion"**
- [ ] **Publish Retrospective:** "We digitized [X] books in week one. Here is what we learned about AI and old paper."
- [ ] **Journalist Follow-up:** Call/Email the journalists who opened your launch email.
- [ ] **B2B Prospecting:** Reach out to 10 other small publishers/libraries with the Criterion Case Study.

**Day 6-7: The "Optimization"**
- [ ] **Automated Onboarding:** Set up a "Nudge" email for users who upload but don't pay.
- [ ] **Feature Roadmap:** Prioritize the next feature (e.g., Batch Uploads, Multi-language support).
- [ ] **Team Retrospective:** Evaluate the "Origin Eyes" (US) and "Criterion" (Nigeria) workflow efficiency.

**🎯 POST-LAUNCH TARGET METRICS:**
*   **Activation Rate:** % of signups who complete their first upload.
*   **Error Rate:** <2% manual correction required per book.
*   **Retention/Repeat:** % of users who upload a second book.

---

### **CONTINGENCY PLANS (The "What If" Guide)**

| Risk | Trigger | Mitigation Plan |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **AI Hallucination** | AI misreads a sacred text/word. | **Immediate:** Add a "Human-in-the-loop" verification step for all orders. **Communication:** Add a disclaimer: "AI-assisted; please review for historical accuracy." |
| **Payment Failure** | Users in Nigeria can't pay $5 via US gateway. | **Immediate:** Integrate a local gateway (Paystack/Flutterwave) or allow "Credit/Invoice" for institutions. |
| **Server Overload** | High volume of large PDF uploads crashes site. | **Immediate:** Implement a "Queue" system. Tell users: "Your book is in line; we will email you when it's ready." |
| **Low Conversion** | High traffic, but $0 revenue. | **Immediate:** Pivot from "Self-Service" to "Managed Service." Offer to do it *for* them for a flat fee. |
