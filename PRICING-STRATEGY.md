# Pricing Strategy — Book Digitization Platform

This pricing strategy is designed to transition your platform from a simple transactional tool into a high-value **Digital Preservation Engine**. Given the mission—preserving Catholic Classics for Criterion Educational Foundation—the pricing must balance **sustainability (for Origin Eyes Inc.)** with **accessibility (for the Foundation).**

---

### 1. PRICING PSYCHOLOGY TACTICS

To move away from a flat $5/book, we will implement a tiered structure that guides the user toward a specific "sweet spot."

#### **A. Anchoring & The 3-Tier Layout**
We will use a "Good-Better-Best" model. The "Better" tier is your actual target.

| **Tier 1: Single Volume** | **Tier 2: Collector’s Pack (Target)** | **Tier 3: Archive Bulk** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **$8.00** / book | **$5.00** / book | **$3.50** / book |
| *Best for testing the AI.* | *Best for small collections.* | *Best for entire libraries.* |
| • 1 Book per transaction | • Min. 20 books | • Min. 200 books |
| • Standard PDF | • Searchable PDF + EPUB | • Full Metadata Tagging |
| • Standard Support | • Priority Processing | • Dedicated Account Manager |

*   **The Anchor:** The $8/book price point makes the $5/book price feel like a significant discount, even though $5 is your intended baseline.

#### **B. The Decoy Effect**
To push users to the **Collector's Pack ($5/book)**, we introduce a "Starter Bundle" that looks mathematically inferior.
*   **Option A (The Decoy):** 5 Books for $40 ($8/book).
*   **Option B (The Target):** 20 Books for $100 ($5/book).
*   **The Math:** The customer sees they can get 4x the books for only 2.5x the price. The "Starter Bundle" exists only to make the "Collector's Pack" look like an unavoidable bargain.

#### **C. Charm Pricing vs. Round Pricing**
*   **Recommendation:** Use **Round Pricing ($5.00)**.
*   **Reasoning:** In the context of religious, educational, and non-profit work, "$4.99" feels like "cheap consumer retail." "$5.00" feels like an honest, transparent, and professional fee. It builds trust with institutional clients like Criterion.

#### **D. Price Framing**
*   **Daily Frame:** "Preserve a classic for less than the cost of a cup of coffee ($0.16/day)."
*   **Annual Frame:** "Digitize your entire 100-book library for just $500/year (Save 20% vs. single purchases)."

#### **E. Loss Aversion**
Don't sell "digitization." Sell "protection against loss."
*   *Frame:* "Physical manuscripts degrade every year. Don't let these sacred texts become unreadable. Secure their digital legacy today before the original copies fail."

### 2. VALUE-BASED PRICING METHODOLOGY

We must price based on the **Economic Value to the Customer (EVC)**.

**The Calculation for Criterion:**
1.  **Time Saved:** Manual transcription of 1 book (200 pages) $\approx$ 40 hours. At $15/hr labor = **$600 value.**
2.  **Revenue Generated:** A physical book sells once. A digital book sells infinitely on Amazon/Kindle. Estimated lifetime revenue gain per book = **$50.**
3.  **Risk Reduction:** The cost of replacing a lost, rare Catholic Classic = **$500+**.

**Total Value Created per book:** $\approx$ $650.
**Pricing Strategy:** If we charge $5, we are charging **<1% of the value created**. This allows you to aggressively lower the price for massive non-profit grants while still maintaining high margins.

Do not compete with Google Lens (the "Value" player). Position yourself as the *only* platform trained to handle the specific typography and aging of classical religious texts.

### 4. FREEMIUM CONVERSION OPTIMIZATION

Since you are transactional, "Freemium" means a **"Proof of Concept"** tier.

*   **The Free Tier:** 1 Book (up to 50 pages) for free.
*   **The Upgrade Trigger:** Once the user sees the perfect OCR (Optical Character Recognition) of their own difficult text, the "Aha Moment" occurs.
*   **The 5 "Aha Moments":**
    1.  **Searchability:** Finding a specific word in a scanned page instantly.
    2.  **Layout Fidelity:** Seeing the original margins/fonts preserved.
    3.  **Speed:** The realization that 100 pages take seconds, not hours.
    4.  **Format Versatility:** Exporting to EPUB and seeing it work on a Kindle.
    5.  **Clarity:** Seeing "noisy" old paper turned into clean, digital white space.

### 5. PRICE TESTING FRAMEWORK

*   **Method: Cohort Testing.** Do not change the price for everyone at once. 
*   **Group A (Control):** Current users see $5.
*   **Group B (Test):** New users from a specific region (e.g., North America) see $7.
*   **Statistical Requirement:** You need at least 100 conversions per group to determine if the difference in "Conversion Rate" is statistically significant (p < 0.05).
*   **Existing Customers:** **Never** raise prices on existing customers. Use "Grandfathering"—tell them, "Because you were an early supporter, your $5 rate is locked in for life."

---

### 6. WILLINGNESS-TO-PAY (WTP) RESEARCH

Use the **Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter**. Send a survey to Foundation stakeholders with these 4 questions:

1.  At what price per book would you consider the service **so expensive** that you would not consider it? (Too Expensive)
2.  At what price would you consider the service **priced so low** that you would feel the quality couldn't be trusted? (Too Cheap)
3.  At what price would you consider the service **starting to get expensive**, but you would still consider it? (Expensive/High Side)
4.  At what price would you consider the service a **great value/bargain**? (Cheap/Good Value)

**The Goal:** Find the "Optimal Price Point" where the number of people who think it's "too expensive" and "too cheap" is minimized.

### 8. MONETIZATION LEVERS BEYOND PRICE

1.  **Premium Metadata/Tagging:** Charge an extra $1/book to have AI automatically tag the books by "Theological Topic," "Author," and "Century."
2.  **API Access:** Charge libraries a monthly fee to plug your digitization engine directly into their existing Digital Asset Management (DAM) systems.
3.  **White-Label Licensing:** Allow other publishers to use your AI engine under *their* brand name for a high annual licensing fee.
