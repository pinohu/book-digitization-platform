# User Research Plan — Book Digitization Platform

This User Research Plan is designed for an AI-driven Book Digitization Platform, focusing on understanding the needs of librarians, archivists, legal professionals, and academic researchers.

---

# User Research Plan: Book Digitization AI Platform

## 1. Research Strategy by Stage

| Stage | Objective | Primary Methods | Key Metrics/Outcomes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Discovery** | Understand current workflows, manual pain points, and AI expectations. | 1:1 Semi-structured Interviews, Diary Studies (7-day) | User Personas, Journey Maps, Problem Statements. |
| **Validation** | Ensure prototypes and AI features (OCR accuracy, layout detection) meet needs. | Moderated Usability Testing, Unmoderated Testing, Surveys | Task Success Rate, SUS Score, Error Rates. |
| **Optimization** | Identify friction in the live product and improve conversion/retention. | Product Analytics, Session Recordings, NPS Surveys | Time-to-Value, Drop-off rates, Feature Adoption. |

---

## 2. Quarterly Research Cadence
To ensure continuous improvement without overwhelming the product team, we follow a **"Rolling Research"** model:
*   **Month 1 (Foundational):** Focus on Discovery. Deep dive into new user segments (e.g., moving from academic to legal users).
*   **Month 2 (Iterative):** Focus on Validation. Testing new UI components or AI model outputs (e.g., table extraction accuracy).
*   **Month 3 (Evaluative):** Focus on Optimization. Analyzing usage patterns and refining the existing UX based on data.

## 4. Usability Testing Protocol

### A. Task Scenarios
*   *Scenario 1 (Upload):* "You have a batch of 20 scanned historical manuscripts. Show me how you would upload these and ensure the AI recognizes the handwriting."
*   *Scenario 2 (Correction):* "The AI misread a date in this document. Walk me through how you would correct this error and save the change."

### B. Execution
*   **Think-Aloud:** Participants are instructed to verbalize their thought process, frustrations, and expectations continuously.
*   **Post-Test SUS Scoring:** Immediately following the test, participants complete the **System Usability Scale (SUS)**:
    *   *10-item questionnaire (Scale 1-5) to measure perceived ease of use.*
    *   *Target Score: >80 (Grade A).*

---

## 5. Interview Guide Template

**I. Warm-up (5 mins)**
*   Introduction (Who we are, purpose of the study).
*   Consent (Recording permission, data privacy).
*   "Tell me a little bit about your current role and how you handle physical documents."

**II. Core Questions (35 mins)**
*   *Workflow:* "Walk me through your process from receiving a physical book to having a digital file."
*   *Pain Points:* "What is the most frustrating part of your current digitization process?"
*   *AI Expectations:* "If a tool could automatically 'understand' a page, what specific details would be most important to you?"
*   *Comparison:* "How does [Current Tool] compare to your ideal workflow?"

**III. Wrap-up (5 mins)**
*   "Is there anything else about your workflow we didn't cover?"
*   Thank you and incentive distribution instructions.

## 6. Synthesis & Analysis

### Synthesis Methods
1.  **Affinity Mapping:** Using Miro/FigJam to group raw observations into themes (e.g., "OCR Accuracy Issues," "Navigation Friction").
2.  **Empathy Maps:** Mapping what users **Say, Do, Think, and Feel** to build deeper persona profiles.
3.  **Insight Statements:** Formulating findings into actionable logic:
    *   *Formula:* "We observed **[Behavior]**, which means **[Insight]**, therefore we should **[Recommendation]**."

---

## 7. Feedback Repository & Sharing

### Repository Structure (e.g., in Dovetail or Notion)
*   **Tagging Taxonomy:**
    *   *User Segment:* (Academic, Legal, Archive)
    *   *Feature Area:* (Upload, Editor, Export, Settings)
    *   *Severity:* (Critical, Major, Minor, Cosmetic)
    *   *Sentiment:* (Positive, Neutral, Negative)
*   **Insight Patterns:** A high-level view of recurring themes across multiple studies.

### Sharing Findings
*   **Research Readouts (Monthly):** A 5-slide deck for Product Managers/Engineers highlighting "Top 3 Wins" and "Top 3 Friction Points."
*   **Insight Newsletters (Quarterly):** A brief, visual email to the whole company (Sales, Marketing, CS) sharing "A Day in the Life of our User."
*   **Searchable Repository:** A central "Source of Truth" where anyone can search for "OCR errors" and see video clips of users struggling.
