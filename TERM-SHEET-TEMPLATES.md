Before proceeding to the templates, a critical structural warning: You mentioned **Origin Eyes Inc. is a 501(c)(3)**. Under US IRS regulations, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit **cannot** own a for-profit company for the purpose of generating profit for its directors, nor can it receive "dividends" from a commercial venture. If Origin Eyes Inc. is intended to be the *investor*, it must be done through a "Program-Related Investment" (PRI) or a separate for-profit vehicle. If Origin Eyes is intended to *own* the platform, you risk losing your tax-exempt status. **I strongly recommend incorporating a separate Delaware C-Corp to hold the IP and receive investment.**

### **1. SAFE TEMPLATE (YC-STYLE POST-MONEY) WITH ANNOTATIONS**

*Note: The "Post-Money" SAFE is the industry standard. It provides investors with certainty regarding their ownership percentage immediately after the SAFE round closes.*

**SIMPLE AGREEMENT FOR FUTURE EQUITY (SAFE)**

**THIS CERTIFIES THAT** in exchange for the payment by [INVESTOR NAME] (the “Investor”) of $[AMOUNT] (the “Purchase Amount”) on [DATE], [COMPANY NAME], a [STATE, e.g., Delaware] Corporation (the “Company”), hereby issues to the Investor the right to certain shares of the Company’s Capital Stock, subject to the terms set forth below.

**1. Events.**
**(a) Equity Financing.** If there is an Equity Financing before the expiration or termination of this instrument, the Company will automatically issue to the Investor a number of shares of Safe Preferred Stock equal to the Purchase Amount divided by the Conversion Price. **[Investor-Friendly: Ensures investor gets equity in the next priced round.]**

**2. Definitions.**
**“Valuation Cap”** means $[VALUATION CAP]. **[Founder-Friendly: A higher cap means less dilution. Investor-Friendly: A lower cap means more equity for the same dollar.]**
**“Discount Rate”** means [e.g., 80%]. **[Investor-Friendly: An 80% discount rate means the investor buys shares at 20% less than the new investors.]**
**“Post-Money Valuation Cap”** means $[AMOUNT].

**3. Conversion Mechanics.**
The Conversion Price shall be the lower of:
(i) The price per share of the Safe Preferred Stock sold in the Equity Financing multiplied by the Discount Rate; or
(ii) The price per share derived from the Post-Money Valuation Cap divided by the Company Capitalization. **[Standard: The investor gets whichever math results in a lower price/more shares.]**

**4. MFN (Most Favored Nation).**
If the Company issues any subsequent SAFE with terms more favorable than these (e.g., a lower Valuation Cap), the Company shall promptly notify the Investor and the Investor shall have the right to amend this SAFE to reflect such terms. **[Investor-Friendly: Protects early investors from being "left behind" if the founder realizes they undervalued the company.]**

**5. Pro-Rata Rights.**
The Investor shall have the right to participate in subsequent Equity Financing rounds to maintain its pro-rata ownership percentage. **[Investor-Friendly: Allows them to "double down" on winners.]**

**6. Dissolution.**
If there is a Liquidation Event before the expiration of this instrument, the Investor will receive the Purchase Amount in preference to the holders of Common Stock. **[Investor-Friendly: Protects principal if the company is sold for pennies.]**

### **2. CONVERTIBLE NOTE TEMPLATE WITH ANNOTATIONS**

*A Convertible Note is debt that converts into equity. Unlike a SAFE, it has a "ticking clock" (Maturity) and carries interest.*

**CONVERTIBLE PROMISSORY NOTE**

**Principal Amount:** $[AMOUNT]
**Interest Rate:** [e.g., 6%] per annum, compounded annually. **[Standard: 2-8%. Higher interest makes the note more "expensive" for founders.]**
**Maturity Date:** [e.g., 24 months from issuance]. **[Founder-Friendly: Longer maturity gives more time to reach a Series A. Investor-Friendly: Shorter maturity forces a conversion or repayment.]**

**1. Conversion at Qualified Financing.**
Upon the closing of a financing round resulting in gross proceeds to the Company of at least $[THRESHOLD, e.g., $1,000,000] (a “Qualified Financing”), the outstanding principal and all accrued interest shall convert into the same securities issued in the Qualified Financing at a discount of [e.g., 20%] or the Valuation Cap of $[AMOUNT], whichever is more favorable to the Holder.

**2. Maturity Conversion.**
If this Note has not been converted prior to the Maturity Date, the Holder may elect to convert the Note into Common Stock at the Valuation Cap or receive repayment of principal and interest. **[Investor-Friendly: Prevents the company from "ignoring" the debt indefinitely.]**

**[NUMERICAL EXAMPLE OF CONVERSION]**
*   **Investment:** $100,000 Note.
*   **Interest:** 5% per year.
*   **Time to Conversion:** 1 year.
*   **Accrued Amount:** $105,000.
*   **Series A Price:** $1.00 per share.
*   **Discount:** 20%.
*   **Conversion Price:** $0.80 ($1.00 * 0.80).
*   **Shares Issued:** $105,000 / $0.80 = **131,250 shares.**

### **3. VALUATION CAP DEEP DIVE (THE MATH)**

The Valuation Cap is the most important term. It sets the maximum "valuation" at which your debt/SAFE converts into equity.

**Scenario:**
*   **Investor SAFE:** $500,000 at an **$8M Post-Money Valuation Cap**.
*   **Series A Round:** Occurs later at a **$20M Pre-Money Valuation**.

**The Calculation:**
1.  **Series A Share Price:** $20,000,000 / Total Pre-Money Shares.
2.  **SAFE Conversion Price:** The SAFE holder uses the Cap, not the Series A price.
3.  **Effective Ownership Calculation:**
    The SAFE holder's price is essentially $8M / (Total Shares).
    The Series A investor's price is $20M / (Total Shares).
    Ratio: $8M / $20M = **0.40**.

**Result:** The SAFE holder gets shares at **40% of the price** the Series A investors are paying.
*   If Series A price is $1.00/share, the SAFE holder gets shares at **$0.40/share**.
*   The SAFE holder receives **$500,000 / $0.40 = 1,250,000 shares.**

**Pre-Money vs. Post-Money SAFE:**
*   **Post-Money SAFE (Modern Standard):** The cap applies to the company's valuation *after* all SAFEs are accounted for. This gives the investor a specific % (e.g., $1M investment on a $10M post-money cap = exactly 10%).
*   **Pre-Money SAFE (Older):** The cap applies to the valuation *before* the new money. This is more dilutive for founders because the "cap" doesn't account for the other SAFEs being issued in the same round.

### **4. DISCOUNT RATE ANALYSIS**

A discount rate is a "reward" for the risk taken by early investors.

**Standard Rates:** 15% to 25%.
**The "Better Of" Rule:** In almost all modern documents, if a SAFE has both a **Valuation Cap** and a **Discount**, the investor gets to choose whichever one results in a **lower share price** (more shares).

**Math Example (20% Discount):**
*   **Series A Price:** $2.00 per share.
*   **Discount:** 20%.
*   **Conversion Price:** $2.00 * (1 - 0.20) = **$1.60**.

**Negotiation Guidance:**
*   **Negotiate Higher Discount (e.g., 25%)** if you are struggling to set a Valuation Cap.
*   **Negotiate Lower Discount (e.g., 10-15%)** if you have a very high Valuation Cap.
*   **Avoid "Double Dipping":** Ensure the document states the discount and cap are not cumulative (you don't apply a 20% discount *to* the capped price).

### **5. MFN CLAUSE DEEP DIVE**

**What it means:** "If you give my friend a better deal next month, I want that deal too."

**Trigger Example:**
1.  **Month 1:** You issue a SAFE to Investor A with a **$10M Cap**.
2.  **Month 3:** You realize $10M was too high. You issue a SAFE to Investor B with a **$5M Cap**.
3.  **The Trigger:** Because Investor A has an **MFN Clause**, Investor A can "activate" it. Their $10M Cap automatically drops to $5M.

**Strategic Implications:**
*   **For Founders:** MFNs make it difficult to "price" your rounds. If you realize you underpriced early investors, you can't easily fix it with later investors without triggering the MFN and diluting yourself further.
*   **For Investors:** It is essential protection against "down rounds" or "cheap" bridge rounds occurring immediately after they invest.

### **7. KEY TERMS GLOSSARY**

1.  **Liquidation Preference:** The order and amount of payout to investors before common stockholders in a sale/liquidation.
2.  **Anti-Dilution (Weighted Average):** A formula that adjusts the conversion price of preferred stock if the company issues new shares at a lower price.
3.  **Drag-Along Rights:** Allows majority shareholders to force minority shareholders to join in the sale of a company.
4.  **Tag-Along Rights:** Allows minority shareholders to join a sale if a majority shareholder sells their stake.
5.  **Information Rights:** The right of investors to receive periodic financial statements and updates.
6.  **Board Composition:** The makeup of the Board of Directors (Founders, Investors, and Independents).
7.  **Protective Provisions:** Veto rights held by investors over certain major company actions (e.g., selling the company, changing the charter).
8.  **No-Shop Clause:** A period during which the founder cannot seek other investment offers.
9.  **Right of First Refusal (ROFR):** The right of the company (or investors) to buy shares before they are sold to a third party.
10. **Co-Sale Rights:** Similar to tag-along; the right to participate in a sale of shares by a founder.
11. **Redemption Rights:** The right for an investor to force the company to buy back their shares at a certain price.
12. **Dividends:** A distribution of a portion of a company's earnings to its shareholders.
13. **Registration Rights:** The right of shareholders to have their shares registered with the SEC for public sale.
14. **Founders' Activities:** Clauses restricting founders from starting other businesses or working elsewhere.
15. **Non-Compete:** An agreement that prevents founders from starting a competing business for a set period.
16. **IP Assignment:** A legal requirement that all work created by founders/employees is owned by the Company.
17. **Representations and Warranties:** Assertions of fact made by the company (e.g., "We own our IP").
18. **Indemnification:** An agreement to compensate a party for loss or damage.
19. **Governing Law:** The jurisdiction (e.g., Delaware) whose laws will interpret the contract.
20. **Pay-to-Play:** A provision that penalizes investors who do not participate in future funding rounds.
21. **Participation Rights:** The right to invest more money in future rounds (often used interchangeably with Pro-Rata).
22. **Conversion Rights:** The right to turn preferred stock or debt into common stock.
23. **Voting Rights:** The right of shareholders to vote on corporate matters.
24. **Preemptive Rights:** The right to maintain a percentage of ownership by purchasing new shares.
25. **Observer Rights:** The right to attend Board meetings without having a vote.
