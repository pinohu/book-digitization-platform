# Crisis Playbook — Book Digitization Platform

This **Crisis Playbook** is designed specifically for the unique cross-border, mission-driven, and tech-heavy nature of your venture (Origin Eyes Inc. in the US supporting Criterion Educational Foundation in Nigeria). 

Because you are handling **Catholic Classics**, your "PR" and "Legal" risks are heightened by the sensitivity of religious texts and intellectual property rights.

---

# 🚨 CRISIS PLAYBOOK: Origin Eyes / Criterion Digitization Platform

## 1. CO-FOUNDER CONFLICT
*Disputes over equity, vision (Mission vs. Profit), or effort.*
* **Early Warning Signs:** Missed strategic meetings, "siloed" decision-making, passive-aggressive communication, or divergence in US/Nigeria operational priorities.
* **Severity:** 5/5 (Can terminate the entity).
* **Immediate Actions (First 24 Hours):**
    1. Call an emergency "No-Blame" summit.
    2. Identify the core friction: Is it *Strategic* (where we are going) or *Operational* (how we work)?
    3. Implement a "Freeze" on major decisions until a mediator is engaged.
* **30-Day Resolution Plan:** 
    1. Engage a neutral third-party mediator.
    2. Review the Operating Agreement/Bylaws.
    3. If irreconcilable: Execute "Buy-Sell" agreement or structured buyout.
* **Estimated Cost Impact:** High ($10k–$50k+ in legal/consulting fees; loss of momentum).

## 2. KEY EMPLOYEE DEPARTURE
*Loss of the lead AI Engineer or the head of Nigerian operations.*
* **Early Warning Signs:** Decreased code commits, sudden PTO requests, or "quiet quitting" behaviors.
* **Severity:** 4/5 (Loss of institutional knowledge).
* **Immediate Actions (First 24 Hours):**
    1. **Knowledge Lockdown:** Revoke/Audit administrative access to repos (GitHub) and sensitive databases.
    2. **Exit Interview:** Attempt to understand the "Why" (Is it money, culture, or a competitor?).
    3. **Internal Comms:** Brief the remaining team to prevent panic.
* **30-Day Resolution Plan:** 
    1. **Counter-offer Assessment:** Only if the person is "irreplaceable."
    2. **Knowledge Transfer:** Mandatory documentation sprint.
    3. **Recruitment:** Activate specialized headhunters for AI/Digitization roles.
* **Estimated Cost Impact:** Moderate (Recruitment costs + 20% productivity dip).

## 3. CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION RISK
*Criterion Foundation provides >25% of total revenue.*
* **Early Warning Signs:** All sales efforts focused on one niche; "Single-client" mindset in roadmap planning.
* **Severity:** 3/5 (High vulnerability to client insolvency or pivot).
* **Immediate Actions (First 24 Hours):**
    1. Audit the current contract for "Termination for Convenience" clauses.
    2. Perform a "Stress Test": What happens if Criterion's budget is cut by 50%?
* **30-Day Resolution Plan:** 
    1. **Diversification Sprint:** Target other religious foundations or academic libraries.
    2. **Relationship Deepening:** Move from "Vendor" to "Strategic Partner" with Criterion to ensure long-term stability.
* **Estimated Cost Impact:** Low (Opportunity cost of diverted sales focus).

## 4. CASH CRUNCH
*Runway < 3 months.*
* **Early Warning Signs:** Delay in vendor payments, dipping cash reserves, declining transactional volume.
* **Severity:** 5/5 (Extinction event).
* **Immediate Actions (First 24 Hours):**
    1. **The 48-Hour Triage:** Categorize all expenses into: *Critical* (Server/Legal), *Important* (Marketing), and *Non-Essential* (Travel/Subscriptions).
    2. **Halt Non-Essential Hiring:** Immediate freeze.
    3. **Cash Call:** Contact major stakeholders/donors for emergency bridge funding.
* **30-Day Resolution Plan:** 
    1. **Revenue Acceleration:** Offer "Pre-pay" discounts to existing customers for bulk digitization.
    2. **Vendor Negotiation:** Request 60-day payment terms instead of 30.
* **Estimated Cost Impact:** Extreme (Potential bankruptcy or massive equity dilution).

## 5. PR DISASTER
*AI misinterprets a sacred text, causing religious offense, or social media backlash.*
* **Early Warning Signs:** Spike in social media mentions; negative comments on digitization accuracy.
* **Severity:** 4/5 (Critical for a mission-driven/religious-adjacent platform).
* **Immediate Actions (First 24 Hours):**
    1. **Designate Spokesperson:** Only the President (Origin Eyes) speaks.
    2. **The "Holding Statement":** "We are aware of the concerns regarding [Issue] and are investigating. Accuracy and respect for the texts are our highest priorities."
    3. **Stop All Scheduled Posts:** Pause all marketing.
* **30-Day Resolution Plan:** 
    1. **Root Cause Analysis:** Was it an AI hallucination or a human error?
    2. **The "Correction" Campaign:** Transparently show how the error was fixed.
    3. **Stakeholder Outreach:** Direct calls to religious leaders/foundation heads.
* **Estimated Cost Impact:** Moderate (Brand damage; potential loss of mission-aligned customers).

## 6. LEGAL THREAT
*Copyright dispute over Catholic Classics or an employee lawsuit.*
* **Early Warning Signs:** Cease & Desist letters; unexpected "Notice of Claim."
* **Severity:** 4/5.
* **Immediate Actions (First 24 Hours):**
    1. **DO NOT RESPOND DIRECTLY:** Anything said can be used in court.
    2. **Preserve Evidence:** Ensure all logs, contracts, and communications are backed up.
    3. **Notify Insurance:** Contact D&O (Directors & Officers) or E&O (Errors & Omissions) carrier.
* **30-Day Resolution Plan:** 
    1. **Counsel Engagement:** Retain specialized Intellectual Property (IP) counsel.
    2. **Risk Assessment:** Is it better to settle or litigate?
* **Estimated Cost Impact:** High (Legal fees can exceed $20k quickly).

## 7. DATA BREACH
*Compromise of customer data or proprietary digitization algorithms.*
* **Early Warning Signs:** Unusual server egress; unauthorized login attempts; customer reports of data leakage.
* **Severity:** 5/5 (Legal and existential risk).
* **Immediate Actions (First 24 Hours):**
    1. **Isolate:** Take affected servers offline to prevent further leakage.
    2. **Forensics:** Hire a cybersecurity firm to identify the breach point.
    3. **Legal Compliance:** Determine if NDPR (Nigeria) or US state laws require immediate notification.
* **30-Day Resolution Plan:** 
    1. **Notification:** Send clear, transparent emails to affected users.
    2. **Remediation:** Patch vulnerabilities and implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across all systems.
* **Estimated Cost Impact:** Extreme (Fines, forensics, and loss of trust).

## 8. COMPETITOR UNDERCUT
*A large player offers digitization at 50% of your price.*
* **Early Warning Signs:** Lost bids; customers asking "Why are you so expensive?"
* **Severity:** 3/5.
* **Immediate Actions (First 24 Hours):**
    1. **Price Audit:** Do not drop price immediately.
    2. **Value Mapping:** List exactly where you win (e.g., "We understand theological nuances that generic AI misses").
* **30-Day Resolution Plan:** 
    1. **Differentiate:** Pivot from "Digitization" to "Curated Digital Theological Archives."
    2. **Tiered Pricing:** Introduce a "Basic" tier to compete on price while keeping a "Premium" tier for accuracy.
* **Estimated Cost Impact:** Moderate (Margin compression).

## 9. REGULATORY CHANGE
*New AI ethics laws or cross-border data transfer restrictions (US-Nigeria).*
* **Early Warning Signs:** News of EU AI Act-style legislation; changes in NITDA (Nigeria) guidelines.
* **Severity:** 3/5.
* **Immediate Actions (First 24 Hours):**
    1. **Compliance Audit:** Map current data flows against the new regulation.
    2. **Legal Briefing:** Consult counsel on "forced" changes to the business model.
* **30-Day Resolution Plan:** 
    1. **Product Pivot:** Adjust AI training/data storage to meet new standards.
    2. **Lobbying/Association:** Join tech-policy groups to influence the regulation.
* **Estimated Cost Impact:** Moderate (Engineering time for compliance).

## 10. MARKET CRASH / RECESSION
*Economic downturn in Nigeria or the US.*
* **Early Warning Signs:** Currency devaluation (Naira); decreased philanthropic spending.
* **Severity:** 4/5.
* **Immediate Actions (First 24 Hours):**
    1. **Cash Conservation Mode:** Move to a "Zero-Based Budget."
    2. **Review Recurring Revenue:** Identify which clients are most likely to cancel.
* **30-Day Resolution Plan:** 
    1. **Counter-Cyclical Pivot:** Target institutions that *must* digitize now to save costs (e.g., moving from physical to digital libraries to save space).
    2. **Hiring Strategy:** Look for top-tier talent from competitors who are downsizing.
* **Estimated Cost Impact:** High (Revenue contraction).
