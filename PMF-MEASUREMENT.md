### 1. SEAN ELLIS TEST (The PMF Survey)
Since your business is transactional, you must survey the decision-makers at Criterion and any staff using the platform to review the digitized outputs.

**The 4 Questions:**
1.  **The Core Question:** "How would you feel if you could no longer use the [Platform Name] to digitize your Catholic Classics?"
    *   *Options:* (a) Very disappointed, (b) Somewhat disappointed, (c) Not disappointed, (d) I no longer use it.
2.  **The Value Driver:** "What is the main benefit you receive from our digitization service?"
    *   *Options:* (a) Speed of conversion, (b) Text accuracy/OCR quality, (c) Preservation of layout/images, (d) Cost-effectiveness.
3.  **The Competitor Benchmark:** "Compared to manual typing or traditional scanning services, how does our AI platform perform?"
    *   *Options:* (a) Much better, (b) Slightly better, (c) About the same, (d) Worse.
4.  **The Gap Analysis:** "What is the one thing we could change to make this service indispensable to your publishing workflow?" (Open-ended).

**Distribution & Requirements:**
*   **How to distribute:** Send via email/WhatsApp to the Criterion administrative team or the specific staff members tasked with proofreading the digitized text.
*   **Sample Size:** Minimum **40 unique responses** from different users/decision-makers.
*   **The 40% Threshold Interpretation:**
    *   **< 40% "Very Disappointed":** **No PMF.** You are a "nice-to-have" tool. You likely have accuracy issues or the $5 price point isn't solving a deep enough pain.
    *   **40% - 60% "Very Disappointed":** **Emerging PMF.** You have a product, but it's leaky. You need to fix friction points (e.g., the upload process or error rates).
    *   **> 60% "Very Disappointed":** **Strong PMF.** You have found a "hair-on-fire" problem. Focus entirely on scaling and operational efficiency.

### 2. PMF SCORING METHODOLOGY (Composite Score)
Because your business relies on volume ($5/book), a single metric isn't enough. Use this weighted formula:

| Signal | Weight | Target (Score 1-10) | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sean Ellis %** | 30% | 8+ | % of users who answered "Very Disappointed." |
| **Retention Rate** | 25% | 9+ | % of books from a batch that are successfully processed without manual rework. |
| **Organic Growth** | 15% | 7+ | Rate at which Criterion asks for more batches or refers other departments/foundations. |
| **NPS** | 15% | 8+ | Net Promoter Score (Promoters minus Detractors). |
| **Usage Frequency** | 15% | 7+ | Number of books uploaded per month (Volume velocity). |

**Composite Score Calculation:** $\sum (\text{Signal Score} \times \text{Weight})$

**Thresholds:**
*   **0–40: No PMF.** (Stop spending on marketing; fix the core AI accuracy).
*   **41–65: Emerging PMF.** (Optimize the workflow; reduce the cost per book).
*   **66–85: Strong PMF.** (Scale operations; look for more publishers).
*   **86–100: Exceptional PMF.** (Aggressive expansion; move from $5/book to enterprise licensing).

### 4. PIVOT-OR-PERSEVERE DECISION TREE

**IF:**
1.  **Sean Ellis Score** stays $< 40\%$ for **4 consecutive weeks** $\rightarrow$ **PIVOT:** Re-evaluate the value prop. Is it the *digitization* they want, or the *searchable database*?
2.  **OCR Accuracy** stays $< 95\%$ for **2 consecutive weeks** $\rightarrow$ **PIVOT:** The AI engine is failing. Stop sales. Re-invest in the technical stack/model training.
3.  **Retention/Repeat Orders** drop below **[X]%** for **3 weeks** $\rightarrow$ **PIVOT:** The $5/book is not providing enough value to justify the effort. Change the pricing or the output format.

**ELSE (IF):**
*   Metrics are stable or improving $\rightarrow$ **PERSEVERE:** Focus on "Tuning the Engine" (reducing your own operational costs to increase margin).

### 5. INNOVATION ACCOUNTING
This ensures you aren't just "busy," but actually "progressing."

*   **Level 1: Establish Baseline (Learning Phase):**
    *   *Goal:* Find the minimum viable accuracy and price.
    *   *Dashboard:* Error rates per book type, time spent per book, manual intervention hours.
*   **Level 2: Tune Engine (Optimization Phase):**
    *   *Goal:* Maximize the margin on the $5/book.
    *   *Dashboard:* AI compute cost per book, human-in-the-loop (HITL) cost, speed of processing.
*   **Level 3: Pivot/Persevere (Scaling Phase):**
    *   *Goal:* Scale to other religious/educational publishers.
    *   *Dashboard:* New customer acquisition rate, LTV (Lifetime Value) of a publishing house, referral rate.

### 6. LEADING VS. LAGGING INDICATORS
*Don't wait for the monthly revenue to tell you if you're failing. Watch these leading indicators.*

| Indicator Type | Metric | Why it predicts PMF 30-60 days ahead |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Leading** | **Queue Depth** | An increase in the number of books "waiting" to be uploaded predicts a revenue spike in 30 days. |
| **Leading** | **Error-to-Page Ratio** | If errors per 100 pages start creeping up, your retention and NPS will crash in 60 days. |
| **Leading** | **"Sample-to-Order" Conv.** | If Criterion asks for a "test run" of a new book type, it predicts expansion into new categories. |
| **Leading** | **Feature Adoption** | If users start using the "Search/Index" feature rather than just downloading PDFs, they are becoming "sticky." |
| **Leading** | **Time-to-Upload** | If the time between "Book Received" and "Book Uploaded" decreases, your integration into their workflow is deepening. |

**Lagging Indicator (The "After-the-Fact" Metric):**
*   **Total Monthly Revenue:** This tells you how you *did*, but by the time it drops, the problem happened 2 months ago. Use the Leading Indicators above to prevent the drop.
