# Scenario Planning — Book Digitization Platform

This scenario planning framework is designed for a B2B/B2B2C AI startup specializing in high-fidelity document digitization (converting unstructured physical/PDF data into structured, LLM-ready knowledge bases).

---

### **Scenario 1: BULL (The "Data Gold Rush")**
**Narrative:** The explosion of Large Language Model (LLM) development creates an insatiable hunger for high-quality, structured training data. Your platform becomes the industry standard for "Ground Truth" data ingestion. Large tech firms and enterprise giants sign multi-year, high-volume API contracts.

*   **Trigger Events:** 
    *   Major LLM provider announces a partnership for proprietary data ingestion.
    *   Breakthrough in "Layout-Aware" AI that allows perfect digitization of complex tables/mathematics.
    *   Global shift toward "Small Language Models" (SLMs) requiring hyper-niche, high-quality datasets.
*   **Key Metrics & Thresholds:** 
    *   **NRR (Net Revenue Retention):** >130%
    *   **API Call Volume:** >10x MoM growth.
    *   **Gross Margin:** >80% (due to extreme automation/scale).
*   **Revenue Impact (Hypothetical Scale):**
    *   **12 Mo:** $2M (Early enterprise wins)
    *   **24 Mo:** $15M (Platform scale)
    *   **36 Mo:** $60M+ (Market dominance)
*   **Required Team Size:** 80–120 (Heavy emphasis on AI Research, DevOps, and Enterprise Sales).
*   **Cash Runway Implications:** High burn rate is acceptable; focus is on "capturing territory" rather than profitability.
*   **Strategic Response Playbook:** 
    *   Aggressive hiring of top-tier AI talent.
    *   Expand infrastructure to handle massive throughput.
    *   Move from "Tool" to "Data Ecosystem" (offering data cleaning and labeling services).

---

### **Scenario 2: BASE (The "Steady Utility")**
**Narrative:** The market views you as a high-quality, specialized tool for specific verticals (Law, Academic Archives, Medical Records). Growth is steady, driven by predictable enterprise sales cycles and mid-market demand. Competition exists but is fragmented.

*   **Trigger Events:** 
    *   Consistent monthly recurring revenue (MRR) growth of 5–8%.
    *   Successful penetration into 2–3 key verticals.
    *   Standardized "Product-Led Growth" (PLG) motion where users sign up for small batches before enterprise upsells.
*   **Key Metrics & Thresholds:** 
    *   **LTV/CAC Ratio:** >3:1
    *   **Churn Rate:** <5% annually.
    *   **Sales Cycle:** 3–6 months.
*   **Revenue Impact (Hypothetical Scale):**
    *   **12 Mo:** $800K
    *   **24 Mo:** $3M
    *   **36 Mo:** $8M
*   **Required Team Size:** 25–40 (Balanced: Product, Sales, Customer Success).
*   **Cash Runway Implications:** Focus on "Unit Economics." Growth must be funded by revenue or efficient Series A/B rounds.
*   **Strategic Response Playbook:** 
    *   Optimize the sales funnel.
    *   Focus on "Verticalization" (building specific features for Legal vs. Medical).
    *   Improve product UX to reduce onboarding friction.

---

### **Scenario 3: BEAR (The "Commodity Trap")**
**Narrative:** Open-source OCR and LLM capabilities become "good enough" for free, driving your margins to zero. Simultaneously, aggressive copyright litigation against AI training data freezes the market. Enterprise sales cycles stretch to 18+ months due to security concerns.

*   **Trigger Events:** 
    *   Major copyright ruling limiting the use of digitized books for AI training.
    *   Open-source models (e.g., Llama-based vision models) match your accuracy for 1/100th the cost.
    *   Customer churn increases as they build "in-house" rudimentary versions.
*   **Key Metrics & Thresholds:** 
    *   **Gross Margin:** <40% (due to price wars).
    *   **CAC Payback Period:** >18 months.
    *   **Win Rate:** Dropping below 20% against open-source/incumbents.
*   **Revenue Impact (Hypothetical Scale):**
    *   **12 Mo:** $400K
    *   **24 Mo:** $600K (Stagnation)
    *   **36 Mo:** $1M (Niche survival)
*   **Required Team Size:** 10–15 (Core engineering and essential ops only).
*   **Cash Runway Implications:** Extreme preservation mode. Every hire must be tied to immediate revenue.
*   **Strategic Response Playbook:** 
    *   **Pivot:** Move away from "General Digitization" toward "Highly Regulated Compliance" (e.g., digitizing documents that require SOC2/HIPAA/GDPR strictness where free tools fail).
    *   **Hard Cut:** Eliminate non-core R&D.

---

### **Strategic Contingency Summary**

#### **The "Bull" Acceleration Tactics (When Green hits):**
1.  **Aggressive Compute Acquisition:** Secure GPU clusters/cloud credits immediately to prevent throughput bottlenecks.
2.  **Land and Expand:** Shift sales compensation to reward "multi-departmental" enterprise deals rather than single-team pilots.
3.  **Acquisition:** Look to buy smaller, niche OCR/Layout-analysis startups to absorb their IP and talent.

#### **The "Bear" Pivot Options (When Red hits):**
1.  **The "Security Moat" Pivot:** Stop selling "digitization" and start selling "Secure Data Ingestion Pipelines" for highly regulated sectors (Defense, Banking, Healthcare) where the value is *privacy*, not just *accuracy*.
2.  **The "Human-in-the-Loop" Service Pivot:** If AI accuracy plateaus, pivot to a "Managed Service" model where AI does 90% and humans do 10%, charging a premium for 99.9% accuracy.
3.  **The "Edge" Pivot:** Move the technology to on-premise/edge hardware for clients who refuse to upload data to the cloud.
