# Customer Development — Book Digitization Platform

This Customer Development Playbook is designed for **Origin Eyes Inc.** to validate the business model of helping **Criterion Educational Foundation** (and similar entities) transition from physical to digital assets using an AI-driven approach.

---

# CUSTOMER DEVELOPMENT PLAYBOOK: Book Digitization Platform

## STEP 1 — CUSTOMER DISCOVERY
*Goal: Validate that the problem (physical decay, lack of access, manual entry costs) is real and painful enough to pay for.*

### 1. Target Customer Personas

| Feature | **Persona A: The Institutional Preservationist** | **Persona B: The Religious Publisher** | **Persona C: The Academic/Theological Librarian** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Demographics** | Heads of Catholic Foundations/Monasteries (Age 45-70). | Owners of small/medium religious publishing houses. | University/Seminary librarians in Africa/Global South. |
| **Psychographics** | Values tradition, legacy, and "saving" sacred knowledge. | Values market expansion, ROI, and modernizing distribution. | Values accessibility, searchability, and catalog accuracy. |
| **Jobs-to-be-Done** | Ensure religious texts survive for the next generation. | Convert physical backstock into digital revenue streams (e-books). | Provide students with searchable, digital access to rare texts. |
| **Pains** | Physical books are decaying; no way to share with the global diaspora. | High cost of manual typing; slow time-to-market for digital versions. | Students cannot find specific quotes; physical books are hard to lend. |
| **Gains** | Global reach for local wisdom; digital archives for posterity. | New revenue via Amazon/Kindle; lower storage costs. | Improved library metrics; enhanced research capabilities. |

### 2. Customer Interview Questions (Problem-Focus)
*Rule: Never ask "Would you use this?" Instead, ask "How do you currently handle X?"*

1. How do you currently manage the preservation of your physical book collection?
2. Tell me about the last time a book was lost or damaged. What happened?
3. What is your current process for making a physical book available to someone in another country?
4. How much time/money does your team spend manually transcribing or cataloging texts?
5. When you want to search for a specific passage in your archives, how do you do it?
6. What are the biggest challenges in moving your current catalog to a digital format?
7. How do you currently decide which books are "worth" digitizing?
8. What have you tried in the past to digitize your materials? (e.g., scanning, manual typing).
9. What was frustrating about those previous attempts?
10. How do you currently track the inventory of your physical books?
11. If a student/customer wants a digital version of a book today, what is the process?
12. How much does it currently cost you to maintain your physical library?
13. What is the impact on your mission when a book is unavailable?
14. How do you currently budget for "technology" or "modernization" projects?
15. Who else in your organization is responsible for the upkeep of these books?

### 3. Problem Validation Experiment (The "Manual Scan" Test)
*   **Objective:** Prove that Criterion (or similar) will actually hand over books and pay for a digital version.
*   **Method:** **The Concierge Approach.** Instead of building AI, use a high-quality mobile scanner and manual OCR (Optical Character Recognition) software.
*   **Budget:** <$500 (Cost of a high-end scanning app subscription + $200 for a freelance data entry clerk).
*   **Timeline:** 2 weeks.
*   **Action:** Approach Criterion. Offer to digitize 10 of their most important books for a total of $50. If they say "Yes" and physically hand you the books, the problem is validated. If they say "It's too much work to ship them to us," you have discovered a *logistics* problem, not just a digitization problem.

## STEP 2 — CUSTOMER VALIDATION
*Goal: Prove that you can sell the product and that people will keep using it.*

### 1. Product-Market Fit (PMF) Criteria
*   **Sean Ellis Target:** >40% of surveyed users say they would be "Very Disappointed" if they could no longer use the platform.
*   **NPS (Net Promoter Score):** Target >50 (indicates high word-of-mouth in religious circles).
*   **Retention Rate:** >80% of publishers who digitize 1 book should return to digitize a second batch within 6 months.
*   **Organic Growth:** At least 20% of new leads come from referrals within the religious/educational community.

### 2. Sales Validation Experiment (The "Wizard of Oz" Sale)
*   **The Setup:** Create a simple landing page describing the "AI Digitization Service."
*   **The Test:** Run an ad or direct outreach. When a customer clicks "Order Digitization," they are taken to a checkout page for $5/book.
*   **The "Wizard":** Behind the scenes, there is no AI. When a payment is made, you manually scan the book and use a standard OCR tool to deliver the file.
*   **Validation:** If people enter credit card details *before* the AI is built, you have a validated business.

### 3. Conversion Funnel Benchmarks
*   **Visit $\rightarrow$ Signup:** 5–10% (Interest in the concept).
*   **Signup $\rightarrow$ Activation (First book uploaded/shipped):** 20% (Commitment to the process).
*   **Activation $\rightarrow$ Payment:** 15% (Willingness to pay the $5 price point).
*   **Payment $\rightarrow$ Retention (Repeat order):** 30% (Proof of long-term value).

## STEP 3 — CUSTOMER CREATION
*Goal: Build a repeatable engine to find and win customers.*

### 1. Demand Generation Strategy
1.  **Partnerships (Primary):** Partner with Catholic Dioceses, Theological Seminaries, and religious NGOs. They act as "aggregators" for many small libraries.
2.  **Content (Secondary):** Create "Thought Leadership" content on "Preserving Sacred Heritage in the Digital Age" (LinkedIn/Email Newsletters).
3.  **Cold Outreach (Tertiary):** Direct, personalized outreach to Foundation Presidents and Library Directors.

### 2. Channel Testing Methodology
*   **Test:** Allocate $500 to **LinkedIn Outreach** (using Sales Navigator) and $500 to **Content/Email Marketing**.
*   **Metric:** Cost Per Qualified Lead (CPQL).
*   **Timeline:** 4 weeks.
*   **Decision Criteria:** The channel with the lowest CPQL and the highest "Intent to Buy" (measured by demo requests or price inquiries) wins the budget for Q2.

### 3. Positioning Statement
"For **Religious Foundations and Publishers** who **struggle to preserve and monetize their physical book archives**, our **AI-Powered Digitization Platform** is a **document transformation service** that **converts physical classics into searchable digital assets for just $5 per book**, unlike **manual transcription services which are slow and expensive or simple scanners which don't provide searchable text.**"

## STEP 4 — COMPANY BUILDING
*Goal: Transition from a "project" to a scalable "company."*

### 1. Transition Triggers (Search $\rightarrow$ Execution)
*   **Repeatability:** You have closed 5+ customers using the same sales pitch.
*   **Predictability:** You know that for every 100 emails sent, you get 2 sales.
*   **Unit Economics:** Your cost to digitize a book (AI + Ops) is significantly lower than $5.

### 2. Scaling Playbook
*   **Processes to Formalize:** Book intake/shipping logistics, AI quality control (QA) workflows, and customer onboarding.
*   **Roles to Hire:** 1. Operations Manager (to handle physical book logistics), 2. AI/Software Engineer (to automate the "Wizard of Oz" steps), 3. Sales/Account Manager (for religious institutions).
*   **Tools to Implement:** CRM (HubSpot), Project Management (Asana), Automated OCR Pipeline (AWS Textract or Google Vision API).

### 3. Pivot Triggers
*   **Price Resistance:** If customers consistently ask for "bulk pricing" below $2/book, pivot to a **SaaS Subscription model** (Monthly fee for unlimited scanning) rather than transactional.
*   **Logistics Failure:** If shipping books from Nigeria to the US is too expensive/risky, pivot to a **"Local Agent" model** (providing the tech to local scanning centers in Nigeria).
*   **Low Value:** If users only want "images" and not "searchable text," pivot from **AI-OCR** to **High-Speed Scanning Services**.

## INTERVIEW LOGISTICS

*   **Where to find them:**
    1.  **LinkedIn:** Search for "Archivist," "Librarian," or "Director" + "Catholic" or "Foundation."
    2.  **Religious Directories:** Databases of Catholic Dioceses and Religious Orders.
    3.  **Academic Directories:** Staff lists of Theological Seminaries.
    4.  **Conferences:** Lists of attendees from Library & Information Science (LIS) or Religious Publishing expos.
    5.  **Direct Network:** Criterion Educational Foundation's existing partners.
*   **Recruitment:** "I am researching how religious foundations preserve their historical texts. I'd love to learn about your challenges. 20 minutes, no sales pitch."
*   **Compensation:** A $25 Amazon Gift Card or a donation in their name to a charity of their choice.
*   **Format:** 20-30 minute Zoom/Google Meet call.
*   **Note-taking Template:**
    *   *Context:* (Who are they? What is their role?)
    *   *Current Workflow:* (How do they do it now?)
    *   *Pain Points:* (Direct quotes of what hurts/costs money.)
    *   *Current Solutions:* (What have they tried?)
    *   *Surprising Insights:* (Anything unexpected?)
*   **Synthesis Framework:** **Thematic Analysis.** Group interview notes into themes (e.g., "Cost is the main barrier," "Shipping is the main fear") to identify the strongest hypothesis to test next.
